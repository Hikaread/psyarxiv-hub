    22тЖТ    22тЖТ18 Abstract
    23тЖТ    23тЖТ19 Background: There is growing evidence that cognitive dysfunction plays a role in the
    24тЖТ    24тЖТ20 emergence and persistence of antisocial behavior, and it is increasingly implicated as relevant for
    25тЖТ    25тЖТ21 risk assessment and treatment within the Risk-Need-Responsivity (RNR) framework. However,
    26тЖТ    26тЖТ22 this evidence comes predominantly from prison and inpatient populations. Whether similar
    27тЖТ    27тЖТ23 patterns hold in forensic outpatient settings remains largely unknown. Moreover, existing studies
    28тЖТ    28тЖТ24 often rely on normative groups that are not matched for age, sex, and education, and tend to
    29тЖТ    29тЖТ25 focus on single cognitive domains rather than administering comprehensive test batteries.
    30тЖТ    30тЖТ26 Methods: This protocol paper describes the design and methods of the FOCUS study,
    31тЖТ    31тЖТ27 which aims to map cognitive functioning in forensic outpatients and examine its associations
    32тЖТ    32тЖТ28 with aggression and treatment outcomes. The study compares forensic outpatients (n = 100) to a
    33тЖТ    33тЖТ29 normative group (n = 100, matched on sex, age, and educational level). All participants complete
    34тЖТ    34тЖТ30 a comprehensive computerized neuropsychological test battery covering seven cognitive
    35тЖТ    35тЖТ31 domains: intelligence, attention, language, executive functioning, social cognition, memory, and
    36тЖТ    36тЖТ32 decision making. Aggression is assessed using both a self-report questionnaire (Reactive
    37тЖТ    37тЖТ33 Proactive Questionnaire; RPQ) and a behavioral task (Competitive Reaction Time Task; CRTT).
    38тЖТ    38тЖТ34 We have four research aims: (1) to compare the cognitive profile of forensic outpatients to a
    39тЖТ    39тЖТ35 matched normative group; (2) to examine the correspondence between self-reported and task-
    40тЖТ    40тЖТ36 based aggression; (3) to examine associations between neuropsychological test scores and
    41тЖТ    41тЖТ37 aggression; and (4) to examine associations between neuropsychological test scores and
    42тЖТ    42тЖТ38 treatment outcomes, including recidivism risk, treatment non-attendance, dropout, and actual
    43тЖТ    43тЖТ39 recidivism.
    44тЖТ    44тЖТ2
    45тЖТ    45тЖТ
    46тЖТ    46тЖТ40 Discussion: The FOCUS study is the first to administer a comprehensive
    47тЖТ    47тЖТ41 neuropsychological test battery in a forensic outpatient sample with a matched normative
    48тЖТ    48тЖТ42 comparison group. Findings are expected to advance understanding of cognitive functioning
    49тЖТ    49тЖТ43 within the RNR framework and to inform more individualized forensic treatment planning.
    50тЖТ    50тЖТ44 Introduction
    51тЖТ    51тЖТ45 Studies from various disciplines such as neuropsychology, developmental psychology,
    52тЖТ    52тЖТ46 and neuropsychiatry have demonstrated that cognitive functioning plays a role in the emergence
    53тЖТ    53тЖТ47 and persistence of antisocial and offending behavior (1тАУ5). The term 'offending' refers to
    54тЖТ    54тЖТ48 behaviors that violate laws, regulations, or societal standards, which may result in legal
    55тЖТ    55тЖТ49 consequences. In this study, we focus on individuals receiving forensic outpatient treatment,
    56тЖТ    56тЖТ50 including those who have engaged in offending behavior as well as those considered at risk of
    57тЖТ    57тЖТ51 doing so. Individuals in offender populations are more likely than the general population to
    58тЖТ    58тЖТ52 experience cognitive difficulties, including impairments in attention, executive functions,
    59тЖТ    59тЖТ53 memory, language, and social cognition (2,6тАУ10). These difficulties are also reflected in the
    60тЖТ    60тЖТ54 overrepresentation of conditions such as acquired brain injury, intellectual disability, ADHD, and
    61тЖТ    61тЖТ55 externalizing disorders in offender populations (11тАУ15).
    62тЖТ    62тЖТ56 Within forensic mental health care, there is a growing awareness of the potential
    63тЖТ    63тЖТ57 relevance of cognitive functioning within the Risk-Need-Responsivity framework (RNR) (16).
    64тЖТ    64тЖТ58 The RNR-model remains the dominant framework shaping forensic treatment. This model
    65тЖТ    65тЖТ59 describes three key principles of effective offender rehabilitation. First, the Risk principle
    66тЖТ    66тЖТ60 emphasizes aligning treatment intensity and duration with the individualтАЩs risk of recidivism.
    67тЖТ    67тЖТ61 Second, the Need principle targets dynamic risk factors that contribute to offending behavior.
    68тЖТ    68тЖТ3
    69тЖТ    69тЖТ
    70тЖТ    70тЖТ62 The third principle, Responsivity, focuses on tailoring interventions to individual characteristics
    71тЖТ    71тЖТ63 that affect the success of treatment, such as learning style, motivation, or cognitive abilities.
    72тЖТ    72тЖТ64 Regarding the risk principle, there is preliminary evidence that incorporating
    73тЖТ    73тЖТ65 neurocognitive measures may improve the predictive value of risk assessment (17,18). Although
    74тЖТ    74тЖТ66 current risk assessment tools incorporate diverse psychological and social criminogenic factors,
    75тЖТ    75тЖТ67 their predictive accuracy remains modest, with area-under-the-curve values typically around 0.70
    76тЖТ    76тЖТ68 (19). Cognitive functioning is thought to underlie several of the Central Eight criminogenic
    77тЖТ    77тЖТ69 needs, the eight most consistent predictors of recidivism (20), by influencing the behavioral
    78тЖТ    78тЖТ70 patterns and self-regulatory processes that these needs reflect. The Central Eight include history
    79тЖТ    79тЖТ71 of antisocial behavior, antisocial personality patterns, antisocial attitudes, antisocial associates,
    80тЖТ    80тЖТ72 and difficulties in the domains of family, work, leisure, and substance use. For example,
    81тЖТ    81тЖТ73 executive functioning difficulties - including poor inhibition, working memory, and cognitive
    82тЖТ    82тЖТ74 flexibility - are associated with the self-regulation problems, impulsivity, and difficulty learning
    83тЖТ    83тЖТ75 from feedback that underlie antisocial behavior and substance abuse (20тАУ22). Language and
    84тЖТ    84тЖТ76 learning difficulties may also affect the ability to process feedback, understand the consequences
    85тЖТ    85тЖТ77 of one's actions, and engage in pro-social communication (14,23). However, domains such as
    86тЖТ    86тЖТ78 social cognition are not explicitly captured within the Central Eight framework, suggesting that
    87тЖТ    87тЖТ79 current risk assessment practice may insufficiently account for the full range of cognitive
    88тЖТ    88тЖТ80 difficulties relevant to offending behavior. Regarding the Responsivity principle, difficulties in
    89тЖТ    89тЖТ81 cognitive functions like attention, memory, and language processing may hinder patientsтАЩ
    90тЖТ    90тЖТ82 treatment responsiveness by limiting their ability to follow structured interventions or engage
    91тЖТ    91тЖТ83 meaningfully in treatment (24), potentially resulting in higher rates of no-show or dropout. In
    92тЖТ    92тЖТ84 short, while the RNR-model and the Central Eight provide a robust framework for forensic
    93тЖТ    93тЖТ4
    94тЖТ    94тЖТ
    95тЖТ    95тЖТ85 treatment, it remains unclear whether a more explicit integration of cognitive functioning would
    96тЖТ    96тЖТ86 improve the accuracy of risk assessment or the effectiveness of treatment. The current study aims
    97тЖТ    97тЖТ87 to address this question.
    98тЖТ    98тЖТ88 Before examining how cognitive functioning relates to risk assessment and treatment
    99тЖТ    99тЖТ89 outcomes, a basic empirical understanding of cognitive functioning in forensic outpatient
   100тЖТ   100тЖТ90 populations is needed. The few studies conducted in forensic outpatients suggest cognitive
   101тЖТ   101тЖТ91 difficulties may be less prominent than in prison and inpatient samples (25), though the
   102тЖТ   102тЖТ92 outpatient population remains understudied (6). In addition, many studies use non-offending
   103тЖТ   103тЖТ93 normative groups that are not matched for age, gender and education, while it is well known that
   104тЖТ   104тЖТ94 these variables are related to test performance (26). Furthermore, few studies have administered
   105тЖТ   105тЖТ95 comprehensive test batteries covering multiple cognitive domains. As a result, there is currently
   106тЖТ   106тЖТ96 no clear picture of how cognitive functioning is distributed across forensic outpatients.
   107тЖТ   107тЖТ97 Cognitive functioning is also thought to be relevant for understanding aggression, with
   108тЖТ   108тЖТ98 difficulties in social cognition and executive functioning linked to hostile attributions and poor
   109тЖТ   109тЖТ99 behavioral regulation (27тАУ31). However, measuring aggression in forensic populations presents
   110тЖТ   110тЖТ100 its own challenges. Most studies rely exclusively on self-report measures of aggression, which
   111тЖТ   111тЖТ101 may underestimate true levels due to social desirability bias and limited self-insight (32,33).
   112тЖТ   112тЖТ102 Behavioral tasks offer a complementary approach; however, correlations between self-reported
   113тЖТ   113тЖТ103 and behaviorally measured aggression tend to be modest in general populations (r = .16-.42)
   114тЖТ   114тЖТ104 (34тАУ36), suggesting that these measures tap partially distinct aspects of aggression. Using both
   115тЖТ   115тЖТ105 measures is therefore informative: it allows us to examine not only levels of aggression but also
   116тЖТ   116тЖТ106 the degree to which self-report and behavioral assessment correlate.
   117тЖТ   117тЖТ5
   118тЖТ   118тЖТ
   119тЖТ   119тЖТ107 The current study addresses these gaps by systematically assessing cognitive functioning
   120тЖТ   120тЖТ108 in a sample of adult forensic outpatients and comparing their performance to an age, gender and
   121тЖТ   121тЖТ109 education matched normative group without a criminal history. In addition, associations between
   122тЖТ   122тЖТ110 cognitive test performance and aggression will be examined, as well as the potential of cognitive
   123тЖТ   123тЖТ111 functioning to inform treatment-related outcomes including recidivism risk, changes in
   124тЖТ   124тЖТ112 recidivism risk over the course of treatment, actual recidivism, no-shows, and dropout. By
   125тЖТ   125тЖТ113 linking cognitive profiles to these outcomes, the study aims to enhance understanding of the role
   126тЖТ   126тЖТ114 of cognitive functioning within the Risk-Need-Responsivity framework in forensic mental health
   127тЖТ   127тЖТ115 care.
   128тЖТ   128тЖТ116 More specifically, this study aims to answer four research questions:
   129тЖТ   129тЖТ117 1. How does the cognitive functioning profile of forensic outpatients compare to a
   130тЖТ   130тЖТ118 normative group without a criminal history?
   131тЖТ   131тЖТ119 2. What is the correspondence between self-reported aggression and aggression measured
   132тЖТ   132тЖТ120 using a behavioral task?
   133тЖТ   133тЖТ121 3. Are neuropsychological test scores associated with levels of aggression?
   134тЖТ   134тЖТ122 4. Are neuropsychological test scores associated with treatment related outcomes (i.e., risk
   135тЖТ   135тЖТ123 of recidivism, changes in recidivism-risk over time, actual recidivism, dropout, and no-
   136тЖТ   136тЖТ124 show)?
   137тЖТ   137тЖТ125 Regarding RQ1, we hypothesize that forensic outpatients will more frequently show a
   138тЖТ   138тЖТ126 deviant cognitive profile as determined using Multivariate Normative Comparisons (MNC), a
   139тЖТ   139тЖТ127 method that evaluates whether an individual's pattern of test scores deviates significantly from a
   140тЖТ   140тЖТ128 normative reference group. Based on prior literature, particular deviations are expected in
   141тЖТ   141тЖТ129 executive functioning and social cognition. Regarding RQ2, we expect to find small to medium
   142тЖТ   142тЖТ6
   143тЖТ   143тЖТ
   144тЖТ   144тЖТ130 (r = 0.20-0.40) correlations between self-reported aggression (Reactive Proactive Questionnaire;
   145тЖТ   145тЖТ131 RPQ) (37) and task-based aggression (Competitive Reaction Time Task; CRTT) (38), indicating
   146тЖТ   146тЖТ132 partial but not total overlap between the two measures. Regarding RQ3, we hypothesize that
   147тЖТ   147тЖТ133 lower neuropsychological test performance will be associated with higher levels of aggression.
   148тЖТ   148тЖТ134 Regarding RQ4, given the limited statistical power of these analyses, the following hypotheses
   149тЖТ   149тЖТ135 are considered exploratory: lower cognitive test performance is expected to be associated with
   150тЖТ   150тЖТ136 higher levels of dropout and no-show, higher recidivism risk, smaller reductions in recidivism
   151тЖТ   151тЖТ137 risk during treatment, and higher levels of actual recidivism.
   152тЖТ   152тЖТ138 Materials and Methods
   153тЖТ   153тЖТ139 The FOCUS project was preregistered in four separate preregistrations to ensure clear
   154тЖТ   154тЖТ140 specification of each research aim, with corresponding hypotheses and analysis plans
   155тЖТ   155тЖТ141 (AsPredicted RQ1: #258783, RQ2: #258784, RQ3: #258785, RQ4: #258788).
   156тЖТ   156тЖТ142 Study population
   157тЖТ   157тЖТ143 The study consists of two participant groups. The first group is recruited at a Dutch
   158тЖТ   158тЖТ144 outpatient forensic treatment center (N = 100). At this clinic, outpatients are treated who have
   159тЖТ   159тЖТ145 displayed criminal behavior or are at risk to do so. General exclusion criteria for treatment at the
   160тЖТ   160тЖТ146 treatment center are: 1) sensitivity to crisis, liability to act out dangerously towards oneself or
   161тЖТ   161тЖТ147 others, requiring immediate intervention, e.g. suicidality, serious self-harm, psychosis, 2)
   162тЖТ   162тЖТ148 substance abuse of a severity that requires clinical treatment or which therapists deem to need
   163тЖТ   163тЖТ149 treatment before any other interventions take place, and 3) offenders who commit crimes from a
   164тЖТ   164тЖТ150 psychosis are referred to a psychiatry center or clinic.
   165тЖТ   165тЖТ151 The second group is a normative group without a criminal history (N = 100). The
   166тЖТ   166тЖТ152 normative sample is recruited using frequency matching to ensure that the distribution of sex,
   167тЖТ   167тЖТ7
   168тЖТ   168тЖТ
   169тЖТ   169тЖТ153 age (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45-54, 55-64, тЙе65), and educational level (low, medium, high) is
   170тЖТ   170тЖТ154 comparable between groups. If perfect cell-level matching cannot be achieved, overall group-
   171тЖТ   171тЖТ155 level equivalence on these variables will be verified, and all analyses will statistically control for
   172тЖТ   172тЖТ156 age, sex, and educational level to account for any differences. The normative group is recruited
   173тЖТ   173тЖТ157 through social media, the networks of researchers and research assistants, and the snowballing
   174тЖТ   174тЖТ158 approach.
   175тЖТ   175тЖТ159 Inclusion and exclusion criteria
   176тЖТ   176тЖТ160 All participants must have sufficient command of the Dutch language and must be 18
   177тЖТ   177тЖТ161 years of age or older. Exclusion criteria for the FOCUS study are: intellectual disability, severe
   178тЖТ   178тЖТ162 substance use disorder (defined as inability to abstain from alcohol or illicit drugs for 24 hours
   179тЖТ   179тЖТ163 prior to assessment), and visual or auditory impairments that preclude participation. Eligibility is
   180тЖТ   180тЖТ164 determined in consultation with the therapist. Participants are asked to refrain from alcohol and
   181тЖТ   181тЖТ165 drugs in the 24 hours before assessment; self-reported compliance is verified in the general
   182тЖТ   182тЖТ166 questionnaire.
   183тЖТ   183тЖТ167 Procedures
   184тЖТ   184тЖТ168 A neuropsychological test battery is administered using the testing platform Mindsware
   185тЖТ   185тЖТ169 (39,40), a computerized assessment platform designed for standardized neuropsychological
   186тЖТ   186тЖТ170 testing. The test battery was constructed based on a systematic selection procedure. Cognitive
   187тЖТ   187тЖТ171 domains were included if they were rated as at least moderately important by a forensic
   188тЖТ   188тЖТ172 neuropsychological expert panel and demonstrated a meta-analytic effect size of тЙе 0.25 in
   189тЖТ   189тЖТ173 offender populations [masked for blind review; masked for blind review]. Tests were selected
   190тЖТ   190тЖТ174 based on meta-analytic effect sizes, expert ratings, suitability for digital administration, and
   191тЖТ   191тЖТ175 psychometric properties. The final battery was reviewed by panels of forensic
   192тЖТ   192тЖТ8
   193тЖТ   193тЖТ
   194тЖТ   194тЖТ176 neuropsychologists and clinical neuropsychological researchers. To minimize effect of fatigue
   195тЖТ   195тЖТ177 and declined motivation, participants are assigned to one of three task orders (see Supplementary
   196тЖТ   196тЖТ178 Table S1). The test administration is done by the principal researcher and/or a research assistant.
   197тЖТ   197тЖТ179 Total administration time is 120-180 minutes. As neuropsychological testing may be experienced
   198тЖТ   198тЖТ180 as demanding, participants are permitted to take breaks as needed or to complete the assessment
   199тЖТ   199тЖТ181 across multiple sessions. During the test administration, the researcher stays present to answer
   200тЖТ   200тЖТ182 questions. To ensure standardized administration, the researcher does not provide additional task
   201тЖТ   201тЖТ183 instructions or explanations beyond repeating the on-screen instructions. The test administrator
   202тЖТ   202тЖТ184 writes down observations or any technical difficulties that might occur.

   273тЖТ   273тЖТ194 To measure risk of recidivism, the Forensic Outpatient Risk Evaluation (FORE) (59) is scored by

   288тЖТ   288тЖТ209 hours in advance or does not attend without notice or permission. Because participants are
   289тЖТ   289тЖТ210 recruited at different stages of treatment, the number of post-assessment sessions will vary

   305тЖТ   305тЖТ224 Statistical analysis
   306тЖТ   306тЖТ225 The analyses will be conducted in R (60). Full analysis details, including data
   307тЖТ   307тЖТ226 preprocessing, outlier handling, and assumption checks, are specified in the preregistrations
   308тЖТ   308тЖТ227 (AsPredicted #258783, #258784, #258785, #258788). Assumptions for the intended analyses
   309тЖТ   309тЖТ228 will be checked. In case of multiple comparisons, Bonferroni-Holm correction (RQ1) or False
   310тЖТ   310тЖТ229 Discovery Rate (FDR) correction (RQ2, 3 and 4) will be applied.
   311тЖТ   311тЖТ230 RQ1: Multivariate Normative Comparisons
   312тЖТ   312тЖТ231 To compare the cognitive functioning of both groups, Multivariate Normative
   313тЖТ   313тЖТ232 Comparisons (MNC) will be conducted. Our approach is based on previously described methods,
   314тЖТ   314тЖТ233 applying MNC to identify deviant cognitive profiles at the individual level (61,62). The
   315тЖТ   315тЖТ234 percentage of deviant profiles in the forensic and normative group will be compared using a chi-
   316тЖТ   316тЖТ12
   317тЖТ   317тЖТ
   318тЖТ   318тЖТ235 square test or FisherтАЩs exact test. Multiple Case Series Analysis (MCSA) (63) will be conducted
   319тЖТ   319тЖТ236 to test the hypothesis that forensic outpatients will more frequently show deviant performance on
   320тЖТ   320тЖТ237 individual neuropsychological tests than individuals without a criminal history.
   321тЖТ   321тЖТ238 RQ2: Correspondence aggression measures
   322тЖТ   322тЖТ239 Pearson correlations will be analyzed between RPQ (total, reactive, proactive) and CRTT (total,
   323тЖТ   323тЖТ240 reactive, proactive) scores.
   324тЖТ   324тЖТ241 RQ3: Regression analyses тАУ aggression
   325тЖТ   325тЖТ242 Multivariate multiple regression will be conducted with the four aggression outcomes
   326тЖТ   326тЖТ243 (RPQ-reactive, RPQ-proactive, CRTT-reactive, CRTT-proactive) as dependent variables.
   327тЖТ   327тЖТ244 Cognitive domain composites will be entered as primary predictors, followed by age, sex, and
   328тЖТ   328тЖТ245 education as covariates.
   329тЖТ   329тЖТ246 RQ4: Regression analyses тАУ treatment outcomes
   330тЖТ   330тЖТ247 Separate regression analyses will be conducted for each treatment outcome, with
   331тЖТ   331тЖТ248 cognitive domain composites as predictors (step 1) and age, sex, and education as covariates
   332тЖТ   332тЖТ249 (step 2). Four treatment outcomes will be analyzed. First, baseline recidivism risk will be
   333тЖТ   333тЖТ250 examined using linear regression with the FORE risk score as the dependent variable. Second,
   334тЖТ   334тЖТ251 change in recidivism risk over the course of treatment will be examined using a linear mixed-
   335тЖТ   335тЖТ252 effects model, with time (baseline vs. follow-up) as a within-subjects factor, a random intercept
   336тЖТ   336тЖТ253 for participant, and time ├Ч cognitive composite interaction terms to test whether lower cognitive
   337тЖТ   337тЖТ254 performance is related to smaller risk reduction. Third, treatment no-show will be examined
   338тЖТ   338тЖТ255 using linear regression with percentage of missed appointments (after cognitive testing) as the
   339тЖТ   339тЖТ256 outcome. Fourth, treatment dropout will be examined using logistic regression. Finally, actual
   340тЖТ   340тЖТ257 recidivism will be examined at a later stage using survival analysis (Cox proportional hazards
   341тЖТ   341тЖТ13
   342тЖТ   342тЖТ
   343тЖТ   343тЖТ258 regression), with time to recidivism since the start of treatment as the dependent variable, once
   344тЖТ   344тЖТ259 sufficient follow-up time (2.5 years) has passed.

   363тЖТ   363тЖТ278 Power and Sample Size
   364тЖТ   364тЖТ279 For multivariate normative comparisons a large sample size (i.e. тЙе 100) is preferred (61).
   365тЖТ   365тЖТ280 Therefore, we aim to recruit 100 forensic outpatients and 100 participants in the normative
   366тЖТ   366тЖТ14
   367тЖТ   367тЖТ
   368тЖТ   368тЖТ281 group. For the regression analyses (RQ3 and RQ4), no comparable regression-based effect size
   369тЖТ   369тЖТ282 estimate is available in the literature; we therefore assume f┬▓ = 0.10 as a small-to-medium
   370тЖТ   370тЖТ283 benchmark (64). Post-hoc power analysis (65) indicated adequate power for RQ3 (N = 200;
   371тЖТ   371тЖТ284 power = .87) but limited power for RQ4 (N = 100; power = 0.50). RQ4 analyses should therefore
   372тЖТ   372тЖТ285 be interpreted as exploratory and hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory. Where
   373тЖТ   373тЖТ286 necessary, domain composites may be merged to reduce the number of predictors and improve
   374тЖТ   374тЖТ287 power, consistent with the preregistrations.
   375тЖТ   375тЖТ288 Data collection started in May 2025 (normative group) and September 2025 (forensic
   376тЖТ   376тЖТ289 group) and is planned to continue until the end of 2026, or until 100 participants are included in
   377тЖТ   377тЖТ290 both groups. The first publication on study outcomes is expected in 2027.
   378тЖТ   378тЖТ291 Missing Data
   379тЖТ   379тЖТ292 All questions and tests in Mindsware are mandatory to ensure complete data collection
   380тЖТ   380тЖТ293 and valid multivariate analysis. If variables are missing, they will be imputed using full
   381тЖТ   381тЖТ294 information maximum likelihood (FIML) estimation in a multilevel framework, as recommended
   382тЖТ   382тЖТ295 previously (66). FIML can be applied as long as sufficient information from other variables is
   383тЖТ   383тЖТ296 available and the missingness is assumed to be at random. If FIML is not feasible for certain
   384тЖТ   384тЖТ297 analyses, multiple imputation may be considered as a backup approach. Participants with fewer
   385тЖТ   385тЖТ298 than 50% of cognitive test scores available will be excluded from analysis. Where appropriate,
   386тЖТ   386тЖТ299 test scores will be adjusted for age, sex, and education level before computing MNC
   387тЖТ   387тЖТ300 classification, consistent with normative correction procedures described previously (62).

   390тЖТ   390тЖТ301 Discussion
   391тЖТ   391тЖТ302 The FOCUS-study is the first to administer a comprehensive digital neuropsychological
   392тЖТ   392тЖТ303 test battery in an outpatient forensic population and matched normative group. In addition, as the
   393тЖТ   393тЖТ304 tests are all computerized, we can standardize instructions and test administration.
   394тЖТ   394тЖТ305 The results of RQ1, which examines the cognitive functioning profile of forensic
   395тЖТ   395тЖТ306 outpatients compared to a matched normative group, will give insight into the extent and nature
   396тЖТ   396тЖТ307 of cognitive difficulties in the forensic outpatient population. Findings may reveal three broad
   397тЖТ   397тЖТ308 patterns. First, cognitive difficulties may be widespread across multiple domains, which would
   398тЖТ   398тЖТ309 argue for more comprehensive assessment as a routine part of forensic treatment planning.
   399тЖТ   399тЖТ310 Second, difficulties may be concentrated in specific domains such as executive functioning or
   400тЖТ   400тЖТ311 social cognition, which would support more targeted assessment protocols and help identify
   401тЖТ   401тЖТ312 which cognitive functions are most relevant for treatment adherence and recidivism risk within
   402тЖТ   402тЖТ313 the RNR framework. Third, cognitive functioning may be generally intact, which would
   403тЖТ   403тЖТ314 challenge the idea that cognitive difficulties are common in this population. Such a finding could
   404тЖТ   404тЖТ315 also suggest that previous studies have overestimated the magnitude of cognitive differences
   405тЖТ   405тЖТ316 between offenders and non-offenders, as many previous studies compare groups that are not
   406тЖТ   406тЖТ317 matched on age, sex, and education (6) and rarely control for other potential confounders such as
   407тЖТ   407тЖТ318 intellectual disabilities, acquired brain injury, socioeconomic status or psychiatric diagnosis. The
   408тЖТ   408тЖТ319 FOCUS study addresses several of these limitations by excluding individuals with intellectual
   409тЖТ   409тЖТ320 disability, using a matched normative group, and measuring psychiatric diagnosis as a covariate.
   410тЖТ   410тЖТ321 However, potential confounding from factors such as socioeconomic status cannot be fully
   411тЖТ   411тЖТ322 excluded.
   412тЖТ   412тЖТ16
   413тЖТ   413тЖТ
   414тЖТ   414тЖТ323 By linking cognitive test scores to aggression, no-show, dropout, and recidivism (risk)
   415тЖТ   415тЖТ324 (RQ3 and RQ4), the FOCUS study contributes to application of the Risk-Need-Responsivity
   416тЖТ   416тЖТ325 model. Despite incorporating diverse psychological and social criminogenic factors, current risk
   417тЖТ   417тЖТ326 assessment tools show modest predictive accuracy (19,67). If cognitive functioning is found to
   418тЖТ   418тЖТ327 predict risk of recidivism, these findings could support the inclusion of neuropsychological
   419тЖТ   419тЖТ328 outcomes in forensic risk assessment instruments. If cognitive difficulties are associated with
   420тЖТ   420тЖТ329 treatment no-show or dropout, they could serve as an early indicator of individuals at risk of
   421тЖТ   421тЖТ330 disengaging from treatment. Conversely, if cognitive functioning does not predict recidivism risk
   422тЖТ   422тЖТ331 or treatment dropout, this does not preclude its relevance for treatment planning. Even in the
   423тЖТ   423тЖТ332 absence of predictive value, cognitive difficulties may still function as a responsivity factor,
   424тЖТ   424тЖТ333 informing how treatment is delivered.
   425тЖТ   425тЖТ334 The comparison between self-reported and behaviorally measured aggression (RQ2) will
   426тЖТ   426тЖТ335 provide methodological guidance for future research. Rather than assuming either measure is
   427тЖТ   427тЖТ336 superior, examining the convergence between self-reported and task-based aggression scores will
   428тЖТ   428тЖТ337 clarify whether these measures capture overlapping or distinct aspects of aggression in forensic
   429тЖТ   429тЖТ338 outpatients, informing which approach is most appropriate for future use.
   430тЖТ   430тЖТ339 Taken together, the FOCUS-project will contribute to a more comprehensive
   431тЖТ   431тЖТ340 understanding of cognitive functioning in forensic outpatients and its relevance for aggression
   432тЖТ   432тЖТ341 and treatment-related outcomes. The findings have the potential to inform both clinical practice
   433тЖТ   433тЖТ342 and research, ultimately contributing to more individualized and effective treatment of
   434тЖТ   434тЖТ343 aggression and other antisocial behavior.
