  1508→essential step toward the prognostic, stratification, and monitoring applications that motivate this work.
  1509→Since its inception in the early 1990s, fMRI-based research has been hampered by small sample sizes and
  1510→significant problems with statistical power277 and the rsfMRI literature mirrors these problems. To address this,
  1511→there has been increased effort to leverage existing datasets and/or organize data sharing amongst established
  1512→research groups. Doing so affords the opportunity to examine the interaction between vital influences on the
  1513→neural response and TBI outcome (e.g., age, time-since-injury). There are now unprecedented opportunities for
  1514→collaborative data sharing through existing datasets (e.g. OpenNeuro, FITBIR, LIMBIC-CENC, CARE) and within
  1515→teams of investigators in the TBI research community.33,39 If rsfMRI is to realize its potential for identifying reliable
  1516→and sensitive markers of neural network functioning, the next generation of studies in TBI must directly examine
  1517→the interacting influences injury (e.g., severity, time post), demographic (age, sex) and contextual (physical and
  1518→mental health) factors that give rise to the wide range of clinical outcomes observed in the TBI patient population.
  1519→To meet this demand, data sharing to increase sample size and representativeness will be essential.
  1520→With increased sample sizes from data sharing, methodological convergence, and clinically informed
  1521→application, rsfMRI remains promising for the non-invasive modeling of neural network response after injury. In
  1522→doing so, the neuroplastic response measured by rsfMRI may serve as a biomarker for staging recovery, identifying
  1523→loci within networks for intervention (e.g., TMS, pharmacologic) and tracking the influence of interactive clinical
  1524→comorbidities.
