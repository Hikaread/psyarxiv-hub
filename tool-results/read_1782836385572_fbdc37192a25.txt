    28‚Üí    28‚Üí    28‚Üí
    29‚Üí    29‚Üí    29‚ÜíPsyHub is curated for a seasoned, practicing psychotherapist who wants research that improves real clinical understanding and therapeutic work.

    31‚Üí    31‚Üí    31‚ÜíThe intended reader:
    32‚Üí    32‚Üí    32‚Üí
    33‚Üí    33‚Üí    33‚Üí- Works primarily with adult clients in private practice.
    34‚Üí    34‚Üí    34‚Üí- Values clinically meaningful findings over academic novelty alone.
    35‚Üí    35‚Üí    35‚Üí- Needs concise but intellectually serious explanations.
    36‚Üí    36‚Üí    36‚Üí- Is interested in complex trauma, mood and anxiety disorders, personality disorders, dissociation, attachment, relationships, sexuality, neurodivergence, psychosis, self-harm, eating disorders, somatic symptoms, addiction, assessment, and psychotherapy.
    37‚Üí    37‚Üí    37‚Üí- Wants findings that deepen case formulation, identify clinically important mechanisms, challenge oversimplified assumptions, improve assessment, or meaningfully inform treatment.
    38‚Üí    38‚Üí    38‚Üí- Does not need generic advice, basic textbook knowledge, superficial wellness findings, or speculative neuroscience.
    39‚Üí    39‚Üí    39‚Üí- Understands that PsyArXiv papers are preprints and should not be presented as settled clinical guidelines.

    41‚Üí    41‚Üí    41‚ÜíSOURCE POLICY
    42‚Üí    42‚Üí    42‚Üí
    43‚Üí    43‚Üí    43‚ÜíPsyArXiv, hosted through OSF, is the primary and required discovery source.
    44‚Üí    44‚Üí    44‚Üí
    45‚Üí    45‚Üí    45‚ÜíDiscover papers using stable APIs wherever possible, preferably the official OSF API and PsyArXiv provider metadata. Do not rely solely on scraping a visually rendered discovery page.
    46‚Üí    46‚Üí    46‚Üí
    47‚Üí    47‚Üí    47‚ÜíThe canonical paper link must point directly to the individual PsyArXiv preprint, for example:
    48‚Üí    48‚Üí    48‚Üí
    49‚Üí    49‚Üí    49‚Üíhttps://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/q73h6_v1
    50‚Üí    50‚Üí    50‚Üí
    51‚Üí    51‚Üí    51‚ÜíDo not use:
    52‚Üí    52‚Üí    52‚Üí
    53‚Üí    53‚Üí    53‚Üí- The PsyArXiv or OSF home page.
    54‚Üí    54‚Üí    54‚Üí- A generic search-results page.
    55‚Üí    55‚Üí    55‚Üí- A fabricated OSF identifier.
    56‚Üí    56‚Üí    56‚Üí- A repository-relative resolver page when the direct link is known.
    57‚Üí    57‚Üí    57‚Üí- A ‚ÄúFind Paper Link‚Äù placeholder.
    58‚Üí    58‚Üí    58‚Üí- A title-based search URL.
    59‚Üí    59‚Üí    59‚Üí- A link copied from another paper.
    60‚Üí    60‚Üí    60‚Üí- A generic `https://osf.io/{id}` link when the canonical PsyArXiv preprint URL is available.
    61‚Üí    61‚Üí    61‚Üí- A direct PDF download URL as the paper‚Äôs primary page link.
    62‚Üí    62‚Üí    62‚Üí
    63‚Üí    63‚Üí    63‚ÜíThe canonical page may contain a download button instead of rendering the PDF inline. That is acceptable. The link must still lead to the exact paper.
    64‚Üí    64‚Üí    64‚Üí
    65‚Üí    65‚Üí    65‚ÜíFor every candidate, obtain and verify:
    66‚Üí    66‚Üí    66‚Üí
    67‚Üí    67‚Üí    67‚Üí- Exact title from the current PsyArXiv/OSF record.
    68‚Üí    68‚Üí    68‚Üí- Complete author list from authoritative metadata.
    69‚Üí    69‚Üí    69‚Üí- OSF GUID.
    70‚Üí    70‚Üí    70‚Üí- Current version number.
    71‚Üí    71‚Üí    71‚Üí- Canonical PsyArXiv preprint URL.
    72‚Üí    72‚Üí    72‚Üí- Direct PDF/download URL for text extraction.
    73‚Üí    73‚Üí    73‚Üí- Initial publication date.
    74‚Üí    74‚Üí    74‚Üí- Most recent version date when available.
    75‚Üí    75‚Üí    75‚Üí- Abstract.
    76‚Üí    76‚Üí    76‚Üí- Full-text content where accessible.
    77‚Üí    77‚Üí    77‚Üí- DOI and journal citation if the paper has subsequently been peer reviewed.
    78‚Üí    78‚Üí    78‚Üí- Whether the record is a duplicate, update, withdrawal, supplement, protocol, registered report, or correction.
    79‚Üí    79‚Üí    79‚Üí
    80‚Üí    80‚Üí    80‚ÜíMetadata verification may use authoritative supporting sources such as:
    81‚Üí    81‚Üí    81‚Üí
    82‚Üí    82‚Üí    82‚Üí- OSF/PsyArXiv API and paper pages.
    83‚Üí    83‚Üí    83‚Üí- Crossref.
    84‚Üí    84‚Üí    84‚Üí- PubMed.
    85‚Üí    85‚Üí    85‚Üí- Europe PMC.
    86‚Üí    86‚Üí    86‚Üí- OpenAlex.
    87‚Üí    87‚Üí    87‚Üí- The official journal or publisher page.
    88‚Üí    88‚Üí    88‚Üí- DOI.org.
    89‚Üí    89‚Üí    89‚Üí
    90‚Üí    90‚Üí    90‚ÜíSupporting databases may verify authors, DOI, publication status, and bibliographic details, but the site‚Äôs primary preprint link must remain the direct PsyArXiv URL.
    91‚Üí    91‚Üí    91‚Üí
    92‚Üí    92‚Üí    92‚ÜíNever infer an OSF identifier from a title. Never assign ‚ÄúUnknown‚Äù as the author merely because one endpoint omitted the author field. Query the paper record, contributors relationship, registration metadata, Crossref, OpenAlex, PubMed, or the PDF title page.     94‚Üí    94‚Üí    94‚ÜíDo not fabricate missing metadata. If an essential fact cannot be verified, place the candidate in a clearly identified review queue rather than publishing invented information.

   105‚Üí   105‚Üí   105‚Üí2. DOI.

   108‚Üí   108‚Üí   108‚Üí
   109‚Üí   109‚Üí   109‚ÜíA revised version of an existing preprint should update the existing entry where appropriate, not create an unrelated duplicate. Preserve stable site identifiers unless the repository explicitly uses another policy.

   113‚Üí   113‚Üí   113‚ÜíCURATION STANDARD
   114‚Üí   114‚Üí   114‚Üí
   115‚Üí   115‚Üí   115‚ÜíEvaluate the actual paper, not merely its title.
   116‚Üí   116‚Üí   116‚Üí
   117‚Üí   117‚Üí   117‚ÜíRead at least:
   118‚Üí   118‚Üí   118‚Üí
   119‚Üí   119‚Üí   119‚Üí- Abstract.
   120‚Üí   120‚Üí   120‚Üí- Research question.
   121‚Üí   121‚Üí   121‚Üí- Population and sample.
   122‚Üí   122‚Üí   122‚Üí- Study design.
   123‚Üí   123‚Üí   123‚Üí- Measures or intervention.
   124‚Üí   124‚Üí   124‚Üí- Main results.
   125‚Üí   125‚Üí   125‚Üí- Limitations.
   126‚Üí   126‚Üí   126‚Üí- Discussion and clinical implications.
   127‚Üí   127‚Üí   127‚Üí
   128‚Üí   128‚Üí   128‚ÜíUse the full paper whenever possible. If only partial text is available, be conservative and do not invent missing methods or findings.
   129‚Üí   129‚Üí   129‚Üí
   130‚Üí   130‚Üí   130‚ÜíSelect only papers with substantial clinical value. A paper is valuable when it does at least one of the following:
   131‚Üí   131‚Üí   131‚Üí
   132‚Üí   132‚Üí   132‚Üí- Changes or sharpens clinical case formulation.
   133‚Üí   133‚Üí   133‚Üí- Identifies a mechanism that matters for assessment or psychotherapy.
   134‚Üí   134‚Üí   134‚Üí- Distinguishes superficially similar presentations.
   135‚Üí   135‚Üí   135‚Üí- Challenges a common but weak clinical assumption.
   136‚Üí   136‚Üí   136‚Üí- Clarifies treatment resistance, dropout, relapse, deterioration, or nonresponse.
   137‚Üí   137‚Üí   137‚Üí- Identifies moderators, mediators, contraindications, or population differences that matter in treatment.
   138‚Üí   138‚Üí   138‚Üí- Offers credible evidence about psychotherapy effectiveness or implementation.
   139‚Üí   139‚Üí   139‚Üí- Improves understanding of attachment, intimacy, conflict, sexuality, trauma, dissociation, suicidality, self-harm, personality organization, mood, anxiety, psychosis, addiction, eating pathology, or somatic distress.
   140‚Üí   140‚Üí   140‚Üí- Improves differential diagnosis or reveals shortcomings in current assessment.
   141‚Üí   141‚Üí   141‚Üí- Provides a clinically meaningful perspective on culture, minority stress, gender, migration, disability, or neurodivergence.
   142‚Üí   142‚Üí   142‚Üí- Introduces a practical therapeutic framework supported by meaningful evidence.
   143‚Üí   143‚Üí   143‚Üí- Synthesizes a clinically important literature rigorously enough to alter practice or conceptualization.
   144‚Üí   144‚Üí   144‚Üí
   145‚Üí   145‚Üí   145‚ÜíDo not select a paper merely because it mentions therapy, mental health, depression, anxiety, trauma, or another relevant keyword.

   147‚Üí   147‚Üí   147‚ÜíPREFERRED RESEARCH
   148‚Üí   148‚Üí   148‚Üí
   149‚Üí   149‚Üí   149‚ÜíGive higher priority to:
   150‚Üí   150‚Üí   150‚Üí
   151‚Üí   151‚Üí   151‚Üí- Psychotherapy process and outcome studies.
   152‚Üí   152‚Üí   152‚Üí- Clinically meaningful longitudinal studies.
   153‚Üí   153‚Üí   153‚Üí- Ecological momentary assessment with clear clinical interpretation.
   154‚Üí   154‚Üí   154‚Üí- Qualitative studies that reveal important patient or therapist experiences.
   155‚Üí   155‚Üí   155‚Üí- Strong systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
   156‚Üí   156‚Üí   156‚Üí- Studies of treatment mechanisms, therapeutic alliance, rupture and repair, dropout, nonresponse, relapse, or adverse effects.
   157‚Üí   157‚Üí   157‚Üí- Research on complex or frequently misunderstood clinical presentations.
   158‚Üí   158‚Üí   158‚Üí- Research with real clinical samples.
   159‚Üí   159‚Üí   159‚Üí- Studies that compare plausible treatment or assessment alternatives.
   160‚Üí   160‚Üí   160‚Üí- Findings with actionable implications for case formulation or therapeutic stance.
   161‚Üí   161‚Üí   161‚Üí- Replications or null findings that meaningfully correct an influential assumption.
   162‚Üí   162‚Üí   162‚Üí- Work that improves culturally responsive psychotherapy without reducing culture to stereotypes.
   163‚Üí   163‚Üí   163‚Üí
   164‚Üí   164‚Üí   164‚ÜíStudies do not need to prescribe a specific intervention. A paper may qualify because it produces a profound conceptual insight that changes how a therapist understands clients.

   166‚Üí   166‚Üí   166‚ÜíEXCLUSION RULES
   167‚Üí   167‚Üí   167‚Üí
   168‚Üí   168‚Üí   168‚ÜíReject papers primarily focused on:
   169‚Üí   169‚Üí   169‚Üí
   170‚Üí   170‚Üí   170‚Üí- Pure theory without meaningful clinical evidence.
   171‚Üí   171‚Üí   171‚Üí- Philosophical or conceptual argument with no substantial clinical application.
   172‚Üí   172‚Üí   172‚Üí- Basic neuroscience, neuroanatomy, neurochemistry, genetics, biomarkers, EEG, fMRI, brain connectivity, or physiological mechanisms unless the paper also provides unusually direct and credible psychotherapy implications.
   173‚Üí   173‚Üí   173‚Üí- Animal models.
   174‚Üí   174‚Üí   174‚Üí- Laboratory perception, attention, memory, or decision-making studies without direct clinical relevance.
   175‚Üí   175‚Üí   175‚Üí- General social psychology.
   176‚Üí   176‚Üí   176‚Üí- Organizational, consumer, political, sports, forensic, or educational psychology without a strong psychotherapy connection.
   177‚Üí   177‚Üí   177‚Üí- Generic student stress, happiness, wellbeing, loneliness, or social-media correlations.
   178‚Üí   178‚Üí   178‚Üí- General population correlations with obvious conclusions and no meaningful effect on clinical understanding.
   179‚Üí   179‚Üí   179‚Üí- Psychometric scale construction or validation unless it materially changes clinical assessment.
   180‚Üí   180‚Üí   180‚Üí- Pure methodology, statistics, open-science commentary, replication debates, or measurement debates.
   181‚Üí   181‚Üí   181‚Üí- Epidemiology that provides prevalence estimates without deeper clinical implications.
   182‚Üí   182‚Üí   182‚Üí- Diagnostic classification debates with no consequence for assessment or treatment.
   183‚Üí   183‚Üí   183‚Üí- Protocols without results.
   184‚Üí   184‚Üí   184‚Üí- Registered reports before results are available.
   185‚Üí   185‚Üí   185‚Üí- Editorials, commentaries, posters, slide decks, corrections, supplements, or withdrawn records.
   186‚Üí   186‚Üí   186‚Üí- Papers whose clinical implications are generic statements added by the curator rather than supported by the results.
   187‚Üí   187‚Üí   187‚Üí- Tiny, severely underpowered studies making broad causal claims.
   188‚Üí   188‚Üí   188‚Üí- Studies whose conclusions substantially exceed their methodology.
   189‚Üí   189‚Üí   189‚Üí- Papers that duplicate an existing PsyHub entry without adding a meaningful revised result.
   190‚Üí   190‚Üí   190‚Üí- Papers that merely confirm standard textbook knowledge.

   205‚Üí   205‚Üí   205‚Üí

   215‚Üí   215‚Üí   215‚Üí- The psychological effects of substance use when directly relevant to assessment or psychotherapy.
   216‚Üí   216‚Üí   216‚Üí- Treating addiction with established non-drug psychotherapeutic approaches.
   217‚Üí   217‚Üí   217‚Üí
   218‚Üí   218‚Üí   218‚ÜíA paper about addiction may be included. A paper whose treatment is psychedelic-assisted or otherwise drug-assisted psychotherapy must be excluded.

   287‚Üí   287‚Üí   287‚ÜíSCORING PROCESS
   288‚Üí   288‚Üí   288‚Üí
   289‚Üí   289‚Üí   289‚ÜíScore each candidate internally before selecting it:
   290‚Üí   290‚Üí   290‚Üí
   291‚Üí   291‚Üí   291‚Üí- Direct relevance to psychotherapy practice: 0‚Äì5.
   292‚Üí   292‚Üí   292‚Üí- Depth of clinical insight: 0‚Äì5.
   293‚Üí   293‚Üí   293‚Üí- Methodological credibility: 0‚Äì5.
   294‚Üí   294‚Üí   294‚Üí- Novelty relative to existing PsyHub entries: 0‚Äì4.
   295‚Üí   295‚Üí   295‚Üí- Usefulness for formulation or treatment: 0‚Äì5.
   296‚Üí   296‚Üí   296‚Üí- Population relevance to the intended audience: 0‚Äì3.
   297‚Üí   297‚Üí   297‚Üí- Confidence in metadata and source verification: 0‚Äì3.
   298‚Üí   298‚Üí   298‚Üí- Risk of overinterpretation: 0 to minus 5.
   299‚Üí   299‚Üí   299‚Üí- Weak or indirect clinical relevance: 0 to minus 5.
   300‚Üí   300‚Üí   300‚Üí
   301‚Üí   301‚Üí   301‚ÜíA high aggregate score does not override a mandatory exclusion rule.
   302‚Üí   302‚Üí   302‚Üí
   303‚Üí   303‚Üí   303‚ÜíSelect the strongest five to ten papers from a normal batch. Select fewer than five if the batch does not contain five genuinely strong papers. Never lower the standard to fill a quota. If none qualify, publish none and record that the batch was reviewed.
   304‚Üí   304‚Üí   304‚Üí
   305‚Üí   305‚Üí   305‚ÜíWRITING REQUIREMENTS
   306‚Üí   306‚Üí   306‚Üí
   307‚Üí   307‚Üí   307‚ÜíWrite for an experienced clinician, not for a general wellness audience and not for an academic peer-review report.
   308‚Üí   308‚Üí   308‚Üí
   309‚Üí   309‚Üí   309‚ÜíEvery statement must be traceable to the paper. Clearly distinguish:
   310‚Üí   310‚Üí   310‚Üí
   311‚Üí   311‚Üí   311‚Üí- What the study directly found.
   312‚Üí   312‚Üí   312‚Üí- What the authors proposed.
   313‚Üí   313‚Üí   313‚Üí- What is a cautious clinical interpretation.
   314‚Üí   314‚Üí   314‚Üí- What remains uncertain.
   315‚Üí   315‚Üí   315‚Üí
   316‚Üí   316‚Üí   316‚ÜíDo not:
   317‚Üí   317‚Üí   317‚Üí
   318‚Üí   318‚Üí   318‚Üí- Invent results, methods, diagnoses, sample characteristics, effect sizes, or implications.
   319‚Üí   319‚Üí   319‚Üí- Convert correlation into causation.
   320‚Üí   320‚Üí   320‚Üí- Present a preprint as established consensus.
   321‚Üí   321‚Üí   321‚Üí- Claim that an intervention ‚Äúworks‚Äù when the design cannot establish efficacy.
   322‚Üí   322‚Üí   322‚Üí- Give individual medical advice.
   323‚Üí   323‚Üí   323‚Üí- Produce promotional or sensational language.
   324‚Üí   324‚Üí   324‚Üí- Use generic claims such as ‚Äútherapists should be aware‚Äù without explaining exactly why.
   325‚Üí   325‚Üí   325‚Üí- Repeat the abstract with different wording.
   326‚Üí   326‚Üí   326‚Üí- Overstate small samples, cross-sectional designs, uncontrolled trials, or exploratory findings.
   327‚Üí   327‚Üí   327‚Üí- Add recommendations unsupported by the study.
   328‚Üí   328‚Üí   328‚Üí- describe a paper as peer reviewed unless its journal publication is verified.
   329‚Üí   329‚Üí   329‚Üí
   330‚Üí   330‚Üí   330‚ÜíUse clear, compact prose. Preserve necessary nuance and limitations.
   331‚Üí   331‚Üí   331‚Üí
   332‚Üí   332‚Üí   332‚ÜíFor each accepted paper, write:
   333‚Üí   333‚Üí   333‚Üí
   334‚Üí   334‚Üí   334‚ÜíSummary:
   335‚Üí   335‚Üí   335‚ÜíDescribe the research question, population, sample where available, methodology, and central findings. Usually two to four substantive sentences.
   336‚Üí   336‚Üí   336‚Üí
   337‚Üí   337‚Üí   337‚ÜíClinical Insight:
   338‚Üí   338‚Üí   338‚ÜíExplain specifically how the findings deepen, reframe, qualify, or challenge clinical understanding. Connect the result to formulation, assessment, therapeutic stance, treatment process, or a clinically meaningful decision. Usually two to four substantive sentences.
   339‚Üí   339‚Üí   339‚Üí
   340‚Üí   340‚Üí   340‚ÜíRelevant For:
   341‚Üí   341‚Üí   341‚ÜíName the clinicians, client presentations, or treatment contexts for which the paper is most useful. Keep this concise and specific.
   342‚Üí   342‚Üí   342‚Üí
   343‚Üí   343‚Üí   343‚ÜíDo not use identical boilerplate across papers.
   344‚Üí   344‚Üí   344‚Üí
   345‚Üí   345‚Üí   345‚ÜíMETADATA AND CURATION FILE FORMAT
   346‚Üí   346‚Üí   346‚Üí
