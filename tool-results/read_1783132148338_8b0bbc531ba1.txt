     7→Social media is one of the widely used channels for interpersonal communication, and
     8→to give personal feedback. However, negative feedback can affect the esteem and mental
     9→health of a person. This study presents a computational network model of a humanoid
    10→agent who gets (an) inappropriate feedback. It explains how he learns to react with his
    11→level of competence in this situation, although he dislikes the feedback. This model can
    12→helptodetectandhandlecyber-aggressionandcanbeusedasabuildingblocktoprovide
    13→support to the victims.

    52→2 State of the Art
    53→This section presents the literature background of reactive aggression in three streams. First,
    54→it will discuss the psychological perspective on how the expression of such feelings helps in
    55→maintaining the mental health of a person. Second, it will discuss the neurological perspective,
    56→which explains the activations in different brain regions regarding the aggressive behavior of a
    57→person. Third, aggression is discussed from the social perspective how such behavior, might
    58→influence a person in a social setup.

    60→threatened egotism [1]. Cyber-aggression is usually less empathetic than face-to-face aggres-
    61→sion, as the aftermaths are not very obvious. Literature shows that feeling angry over the
    62→harmful behavior of another person is a natural and reactive emotion [7, 22, 25]. It is good
    63→to suppress any negative feelings, however, at times it is better to express your anger [7, 25],
    64→becausesuppressingangermaycausementalillness[20]. Amanagedexpressionandacourteous
    65→negotiationmightactuallyhelpotherswhilekeepingone’sself-esteemhigh[7,21]. Apro-social
    66→response is always appreciated and can be learned over a period of time [8].
    67→Whilelookingintothecognitive-neuroscienceperspective,theamygdalaisobservedto“play
    68→a critical role to react over an emotional stimulus”, deciding how to process the information,
    69→in a positive, negative, or neutral way [9]. Unlike in post-traumatic stress, the frontal cortex is
    70→implicated in the regulation of response to a threat. The orientation of this anger is directed
    71→towards punishment, causing activations in the amygdala, pre-frontal and posterior cingulate
    72→cortices. During any threat, the amygdala-hypothalamus and the periaqueductal-gray become
    73→active[4]alongwiththehippocampus. Someneurotransmittersarealsoinvolved. Asanexam-
    74→ple, 5-hydroxytryptamine (5-HT) predisposes an individual to impulsive aggression. Dopamine

    77→in the levels of nor-adrenaline may result in aggressive behaviors of a person.

    79→must learn to manage their physiological and cognitive arousal in a way that they can achieve
    80→some level of competence [8]. Mental processes depict the feelings, emotions, and actions of an
    81→individual. A person can feel and decide for his actions through the prediction of these feelings

   558→6 Conclusion
   559→A temporal-causal network model [23] was presented explaining the influence of negative feed-
   560→back, and a defensive reaction by a humanoid-agent. Threatened egotism is considered one of
   561→the major causes of aggression. Feedback is perceived anonymous in social media, but it has
   562→limitless exposure. If it is negative, it can arouse angry feelings among peers. As a reaction of
   563→angry feelings, an impulsive or cautious response is expected. An impulsive reaction is usually
   564→monitoredbypredictedeffects,whilelearningcanplayanimportantroleinajudiciousreaction.
   565→A cautious or judicious reaction is justified and requires a level of competence. The model was
   566→validatedandtunedusingconversationaldata,anditcanserveasabasistodetectandsupport
   567→anger with reference to the levels of sensitivity of a situation.
   568→In future work, we aim to address the recurrence of anger and analyze it with respect to
   569→behaviorsobservedinsocialmedia. Moreover,weaimtostudyhowthismodelcanbeusedasthe
   570→basistoprovidesupporttothepeers. Wewouldalsoliketoexploremachinelearningtechniques
   571→further that can be helpful in detecting aggression, and devise a supporting mechanism for the
   572→victims and the aggressors.
