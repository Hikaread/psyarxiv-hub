   224‚Üí   273‚Üí   273‚Üí   273‚Üípersonality (Cramer et al., 2012), attitude and beliefs (Brandt et al., 2019; Dalege et al.,
   225‚Üí   274‚Üí   274‚Üí   274‚Üí2018), and mental health (Blanken et al., 2019; Borsboom & Cramer, 2013). A network
   226‚Üí   275‚Üí   275‚Üí   275‚Üíessentially consists of nodes, typically representing entities, and edges that reflect the
   227‚Üí   276‚Üí   276‚Üí   276‚Üírelationships between them. In the current study, the 14 self-control belief components were
   228‚Üí   277‚Üí   277‚Üí   277‚Üíset as nodes. To visualize the associations among them, we applied the EBICglasso model in
   229‚Üí   278‚Üí   278‚Üí   278‚ÜíJASP, which automatically sets small connections to zero, thus reducing relatively weak
   230‚Üí   279‚Üí   279‚Üí   279‚Üíassociations and producing a more parsimonious network (Epskamp & Fried, 2018; Friedman
   231‚Üí   280‚Üí   280‚Üí   280‚Üíet al., 2008). A thick and direct edge represents a strong and direct association between two
   232‚Üí   281‚Üí   281‚Üí   281‚Üínodes. We examined the clustering patterns in the network to identify potential higher-order
   233‚Üí   282‚Üí   282‚Üí   282‚Üíbelief factors (Borsboom et al., 2021; Costantini et al., 2015, 2020; Golino & Epskamp,
   234‚Üí   283‚Üí   283‚Üí   283‚Üí2017). Additionally, we reported node strength, betweenness, and closeness to assess the
   235‚Üí   284‚Üí   284‚Üí   284‚Üícentrality of the self-control belief components. Correlation stability coefficient (CS-
   236‚Üí   285‚Üí   285‚Üí   285‚Üícoefficient) was calculated using the R package bootnet to evaluate the stability of node
   237‚Üí   286‚Üí   286‚Üí   286‚Üícentrality, with a preferred threshold of above 0.5 (Epskamp et al., 2018).

   239‚Üí   288‚Üí   288‚Üí   288‚Üíhow specific items loaded onto the clusters. Factor analysis and network analysis are not
   240‚Üí   289‚Üí   289‚Üí   289‚Üímutually exclusive but rather complementary; while network analysis visualizes patterns of
   241‚Üí   290‚Üí   290‚Üí   290‚Üíassociations, factor analysis helps to identify groups of closely connected components
   242‚Üí   291‚Üí   291‚Üí   291‚Üí(Cramer et al., 2012). This combined approach allowed us to identify potential higher-order
   243‚Üí   292‚Üí   292‚Üí   292‚Üíbelief factors with greater robustness and obtain more accurate scores for each factor based

   245‚Üí   294‚Üí   294‚Üí   294‚Üíminimum factor loading threshold of .40. Factor rotation was set to oblique (oblimin),
   246‚Üí   295‚Üí   295‚Üí   295‚Üíallowing for correlations among factors.
