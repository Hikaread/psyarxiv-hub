   662â†’Given the high intercorrelations among the variables assessed in this study, the
   663â†’author examined the semantic similarity of questionnaire items. All questionnaire items used
   664â†’in this study were evaluated for overlapping descriptions and semantic similarity. Based on
   665â†’this assessment, several item pairs among the questionnaires indicated semantic overlap.
   666â†’For example: â€œHave had difficulty concentrating?â€‌ (IDQ) and â€œHad difficulty concentrating?â€‌
   667â†’(IAQ), â€œFeeling jumpy or easily startled?â€‌ (ITQ) and â€œFelt physically tense or agitated?â€‌ (IAQ),
   668â†’or â€œWhen I am upset, it takes me a long time to calm downâ€‌ (ITQ) and â€œWhen Iâ€™m upset, it
   669â†’takes me a long time to feel betterâ€‌ (DERS). Thus, judged by face validity, multiple
   670â†’measurement instruments employed in this study show inherent similarities in their semantic
   671â†’content. Furthermore, the similarity of questionnaire items was assessed with the Harmony
   672â†’software, using the harmonydata package for R (Wood et al., 2022). Harmony is a free-to-
   673â†’use tool developed for harmonization of mental health questionnaires (McElroy et al., 2024).
   674â†’It employs natural language processing to represent each text question into a numeric vector,
   675â†’and judge its similarity based on its respective distance to other vectors. A cosine similarity
   676â†’index is computed ranging from -1 to +1, with values closer to 1 reflecting a greater semantic
   677â†’match. A moderate correlation was found between the cosine indices of similarity and the
   678â†’Spearman coefficients in a test battery of 39 mental health questions. McElroy et al. (2024)
   679â†’examined a holdout sample of questionnaires, which were not used to train the language
   680â†’
   681â†’19
   682â†’model, and found that the cosine similarity indices could predict the real-world correlation
   683â†’coefficients of questionnaire-items. Notably, the IDQ, IAQ (Shevlin et al., 2023), and ITQ
   684â†’(Cloitre et al., 2018) employed in this study were used in the holdout sample, and similar
   685â†’intercorrelations and correlational networks were found between those questionnaires. Thus,
   686â†’the author implemented Harmony to objectively assess the semantic similarities among the
   687â†’questionnaires used in this study. For analysis, the author disabled matches of item pairs
   688â†’within instruments, permitted multiple matches for a single questionnaire item, and set the
   689â†’cut-off for relevant similarities to a cosine similarity index greater than or equal to .70. The
   690â†’language model produced 11 matches of item pairs with relevant cosine similarity indices
   691â†’(see Appendix D). The matches largely overlap the similarity assessment judged by face
   692â†’validity.
   693â†’Furthermore, post-hoc power analyses revealed less statistical power than a priori
   694â†’anticipated, estimating a power of 48.6% for PTSD, 37.0% for anxiety, 18.3% for DSO, and
   695â†’6.4% for depressive symptoms. Post-hoc power analysis of interaction effects was conducted
   696â†’using the Shiny App InteractionPoweR, an openly available tool to estimate statistical power
   697â†’of moderated multiple linear regression models (Finsaas et al., 2021). A review of 261
   698â†’articles in psychology journals found that interaction effects tend to be quite small with a
   699â†’median effect of r = .04 (Aguinis et al., 2005). In personality science, replicable interaction
   700â†’effects tend to be even smaller with a median effect size of r = .02 (Vize et al., 2022). In
   701â†’consequence, incorrect power estimations may lead to largely underpowered studies.
   702â†’Baranger et al. (2023) described power estimations for interaction effects as a function of
   703â†’residual variance explained by the model, which depends on the correlations between each
   704â†’predictor and the outcome, as well as the correlation of the predictors with each other. While
   705â†’larger correlations of the respective predictors with the outcome can increase the residual
   706â†’variance explained by the interaction term, a larger correlation between the predictors and
   707â†’the interaction term can both decrease or increase statistical power. The influence on power
   708â†’estimations for interaction effects depends on whether the total variance explained by the
   709â†’interaction term is greater or less than the sum of their individual effects (Baranger et al.,
   710â†’2023). In this study, the effects of ED decreased the individual effects of CTS on the outcome
   711â†’variables, leading to less total variance explained by the interaction term and decreased
   712â†’statistical power.
   713â†’Discussion
   714â†’Continuous traumatic stress (CTS) describes the psychological reaction to contexts of
   715â†’multiple potentially traumatic stressors, which are experienced as ongoing and
   716â†’encompassing. The theoretical framework postulates encompassing psychological reactions
   717â†’that are associated with, but distinct from, trauma-related psychopathologies. CTS symptoms
   718â†’largely overlap with ICD-11 diagnostic categories, such as CPTSD and MDD (WHO, 2019).
   719â†’
   720â†’20
   721â†’Nonetheless, symptoms in specific contexts (i.e., civilians in war-threatened regions) may not
   722â†’represent manifest psychopathologies but psychological distress due to ongoing and
   723â†’encompassing traumatic stressors. Moreover, these reactions may constitute a functional
   724â†’reaction to threatful environments (Itzhaky et al., 2017). The WHO would include CTS in the
   725â†’ICD-11 if sufficient empirical evidence were provided (Bachem & Maercker, 2024). Therefore,
   726â†’this study investigated associations between CTS and psychopathological symptoms in two
   727â†’samples of Ukrainian civilians in Austria and Ukraine in 2025. Further, effects of emotion
   728â†’dysregulation (ED) on these associations and group differences in levels of
   729â†’psychopathological symptoms between both subsamples were examined. Exploratory
   730â†’analyses comprised frequencies of questionnaire-derived diagnoses and index traumas, as
   731â†’well as post-hoc power analyses, and semantic similarity assessments of measurement
   732â†’instruments.
   733â†’Findings show considerable associations of CTS with psychopathological symptoms,
   734â†’accounting for 28% to 47% of variance in depressive, anxiety, PTSD, and DSO symptoms.
   735â†’The residence of participants (i.e., Eastern Ukraine vs. Austria) did not explain variance in
   736â†’psychopathological symptoms beyond CTS. ED emerged as an additional clinically relevant
   737â†’factor, predicting depressive, anxiety, and DSO symptoms. Further, ED influenced the
   738â†’associations of CTS with anxiety, PTSD, and DSO symptoms, attenuating these
   739â†’associations. However, the incremental variance explained by the interaction term was small,
   740â†’and post-hoc power analyses indicated that moderation effects were largely underpowered.
   741â†’Contrary to hypotheses, participants residing in Austria reported higher levels of PTSD and
   742â†’DSO symptoms, a greater number of traumatic exposures, and a significantly higher
   743â†’prevalence of questionnaire-derived CPTSD diagnoses. Exploratory analyses revealed that
   744â†’two-thirds (66.4%) of participants met questionnaire-derived diagnostic criteria for one or
   745â†’more ICD-11 disorders. Lastly, high comorbidity of questionnaire-derived diagnoses
   746â†’throughout the sample and large associations between all measurement instruments were
   747â†’observed.
   748â†’The strong overlap within internalizing symptoms aligns with the Hierarchical
   749â†’Taxonomy of Psychopathology (HiTOP) model (Kotov et al., 2017, 2021), suggesting that a
   750â†’general distress factor may underlie the observed symptom patterns and that CTS potentially
   751â†’represents a context-specific construct of this distress dimension. At the same time, the size
   752â†’of observed effects underscores the necessity of a solid theoretical framework, as these
   753â†’associations cannot be interpreted meaningfully from a purely data-driven perspective.
   754â†’Therefore, the mechanisms of these relationships remain unclear. However, all
