    50в†’    74в†’    74в†’any explicit or implicit information about the purpose of the study is likely to affect their
    51в†’    75в†’    75в†’responses and behavior. All the explicit and implicit clues about the purpose of the study are
    52в†’    76в†’    76в†’called вЂњdemand characteristicsвЂќ, because participants use these clues to identify what the
    53в†’    77в†’    77в†’experimenter is вЂњdemandingвЂќ of them. More recently, demand effects were conceptualized in
    54в†’    78в†’    78в†’three sub-dimensions [28]: cue interpretation (i.e., "the analysis and interpretation of these cues
    55в†’    79в†’    79в†’by the participants to produce a set of conjectures about the purpose of the research"),
    56в†’    80в†’    80в†’sensitivity to demand characteristics (i.e., an expression of interest on behalf of the respondent,
    57в†’    81в†’    81в†’seeking to make sense of the questioning situation with which they are confronted"), and
    58в†’    82в†’    82в†’response motivation (i.e., "the willingness of the individuals being questioned to rely on their
    59в†’    83в†’    83в†’interpretation of the demand characteristics in order to engage in specific response behaviors).
    60в†’    84в†’    84в†’Based on these premises, the explicit nature of self-reported craving scales, especially when
    61в†’    85в†’    85в†’administered twice in the same experiment, is likely to induce specific demand effects in the
    62в†’    86в†’    86в†’participants.
    63в†’    87в†’    87в†’In a recent experiment, we investigated the impact of a pre-test measurement of alcohol
    64в†’    88в†’    88в†’craving on its final assessment, in relation to social desirability and demand effects [24].
    65в†’    89в†’    89в†’Participants were randomly allocated to two experimental groups: a вЂњpre-postвЂќ group in which
    66в†’    90в†’    90в†’craving was assessed twice, before and after watching an alcohol-related video; or a вЂњpost-onlyвЂќ
    67в†’    91в†’    91в†’

    69в†’    93в†’    93в†’group in which craving was assessed only after the video. Social desirability, demand effects,
    70в†’    94в†’    94в†’engagement toward the video, and alcohol consumption levels were also assessed using
    71в†’    95в†’    95в†’validated questionnaires. The results showed that the levels of craving after watching an alcohol
    72в†’    96в†’    96в†’video were higher in heavier drinkers relative to low drinkers. Additionally, the pre-test
    73в†’    97в†’    97в†’assessment of craving had a significant impact on post-test craving, but only in heavier alcohol
    74в†’    98в†’    98в†’drinkers. More precisely, in high alcohol drinkers, alcohol craving was significantly higher after
    75в†’    99в†’    99в†’the video when craving was also assessed before the video. No such effect was observed in low
    76в†’   100в†’   100в†’alcohol drinkers. In contrast, no relationship between self-reported craving and SD or demand
    77в†’   101в†’   101в†’effects was found in that study. Finally, exploratory analyses indicated that engagement toward
    78в†’   102в†’   102в†’the alcohol-related video was a significant mediator of subsequent craving increases.
    79в†’   103в†’   103в†’Since this initial study took place during the COVID-19 lockdown period and considering

   194в†’   218в†’   218в†’4. Discussion
   195в†’   219в†’   219в†’The primary objective of this study was to determine whether our initial findings could be
   196в†’   220в†’   220в†’self-replicated outside the context of the covid-19 lockdown. The initial study examined
   197в†’   221в†’   221в†’whether repeated measurements of craving influenced its assessment after watching an alcohol-
   198в†’   222в†’   222в†’related video, considering social desirability and demand effects. The findings of the initial
   199в†’   223в†’   223в†’study revealed significant effects of the repeated measurement of craving, alcohol consumption
   200в†’   224в†’   224в†’level, and their interaction, suggesting that pre-experimental craving measurements elevated
   201в†’   225в†’   225в†’post-experimental levels, but only in heavy drinkers. No evidence was found linking these
   202в†’   226в†’   226в†’
   203в†’   227в†’   227в†’11
   204в†’   228в†’   228в†’changes to social desirability or demand effects. Further analyses also indicated that the increase
   205в†’   229в†’   229в†’in craving in heavy drinkers was mediated by a stronger engagement with the alcohol-related
   206в†’   230в†’   230в†’video.
   207в†’   231в†’   231в†’The present study replicated most results of the initial study, strengthening the robustness
   208в†’   232в†’   232в†’of the initial findings. In both studies, level of alcohol consumption significantly influenced
   209в†’   233в†’   233в†’cue-induced craving, and, when repeated assessments were conducted, craving levels increased
   210в†’   234в†’   234в†’following cue exposure compared to pre-exposure levels, indicating successful craving
   211в†’   235в†’   235в†’induction. Similar effects were documented in the literature over recent decades [25,32вЂ“35].
   212в†’   236в†’   236в†’These results demonstrate that the video cue exposure used in both studies successfully induced
   213в†’   237в†’   237в†’subjective craving and effectively differentiated between light and heavy drinkers.
   214в†’   238в†’   238в†’Notably, the impact of engagement was also replicated, with a correlation of similar
   215в†’   239в†’   239в†’magnitude in the two studies (initial study: r = .44; present study: r = .42). This finding provides
   216в†’   240в†’   240в†’further support for our hypothesis that the level of engagement with alcohol-related stimuli
   217в†’   241в†’   241в†’(whether in a video format or other media) is a critical factor in craving induction.
   218в†’   242в†’   242в†’In both studies, there was no indication of a statistically significant effect of social
   219в†’   243в†’   243в†’desirability, or demand characteristics on cue-induced craving. This indicates that self-reported
   220в†’   244в†’   244в†’alcohol craving was not significantly affected by participants' tendencies to present themselves
   221в†’   245в†’   245в†’in a socially desirable manner or to conform to perceived experimental expectations. The
   222в†’   246в†’   246в†’absence of such biases enhances the validity of the experimental findings on craving, suggesting
   223в†’   247в†’   247в†’that the responses were mainly driven by experimental manipulations, such as alcohol cue
   224в†’   248в†’   248в†’exposure. However, future research could further validate this conclusion by using implicit
   225в†’   249в†’   249в†’measures of craving or incorporating additional checks to detect potential subtle influences of
   226в†’   250в†’   250в†’social desirability and demand characteristics.
   227в†’   251в†’   251в†’
   228в†’   252в†’   252в†’12
   229в†’   253в†’   253в†’Since the effects of repeated measurements of craving were not replicated in the present
   230в†’   254в†’   254в†’study, we questioned what differences between the two studies might explain these
   231в†’   255в†’   255в†’discrepancies. A major difference was the gender ratio. Participants in the second study were
   232в†’   256в†’   256в†’recruited from a "participant pool" in the Faculty of Psychology, resulting in a very high
   233в†’   257в†’   257в†’proportion of female participants, reflecting the faculty's gender distribution. Consequently, we
   234в†’   258в†’   258в†’reanalyzed the data from the initial study to explore potential gender effects. This analysis
   235в†’   259в†’   259в†’revealed a significant gender effect (Figure 3) with a large interaction effect between alcohol
   236в†’   260в†’   260в†’consumption and repeated measurements in men, whereas this effect was absent in women.
   237в†’   261в†’   261в†’Therefore, the two studies yielded similar effects when gender is included in the analyses. We
   238в†’   262в†’   262в†’also checked whether differences in AUDIT-C scores or levels of engagement could act as
   239в†’   263в†’   263в†’confounding variables explaining the observed gender difference; however, this was not the
   240в†’   264в†’   264в†’case in our data (AUDIT-C: W= 745, p=0.35, r= 0.11 CI95% -0.12 - 0.21; Engagement:
   241в†’   265в†’   265в†’W=680.5, p=0.83, r=0.026, CI 95%, -0.22 вЂ“ 0.05). However, these analyses were not part of
   242в†’   266в†’   266в†’the initial study design, and the sample sizes were relatively small (20 women and 15 men in
   243в†’   267в†’   267в†’the pre-post group, and 24 women and 15 men in the post-only group). Therefore, further
   244в†’   268в†’   268в†’research is needed to confirm these conclusions.
   245в†’   269в†’   269в†’This observed gender difference might be related to gender stereotypes. For women, the
   246в†’   270в†’   270в†’reported levels of post-video craving were consistent with their usual alcohol consumption:
   247в†’   271в†’   271в†’light drinkers reported low craving, while heavier drinkers reported higher levels of craving,
   248в†’   272в†’   272в†’regardless of the repeated measurements. In contrast, a different pattern emerged for men. When
   249в†’   273в†’   273в†’craving was assessed only after the video, the reported levels were unrelated to their drinking
   250в†’   274в†’   274в†’habits. Their responses seemed to be directly influenced by the cue exposure itself, potentially
   251в†’   275в†’   275в†’activating social stereotypes that portray heavy drinking as socially positive for men. However,
   252в†’   276в†’   276в†’the pre-video measurement of craving appeared to anchor their reported levels to their drinking
   253в†’   277в†’   277в†’
   254в†’   278в†’   278в†’13
   255в†’   279в†’   279в†’habits, subsequently influencing their post-video responses. This speculative explanation
   256в†’   280в†’   280в†’clearly requires further confirmation in future studies.
   257в†’   281в†’   281в†’In conclusion, the present results suggest that an initial pre-exposure craving measurement
   258в†’   282в†’   282в†’could significantly impact the final craving assessment in men, but not in women. Moreover,
   259в†’   283в†’   283в†’the way individuals engage with alcohol-related videos is significantly related to increased
   260в†’   284в†’   284в†’craving for alcohol afterwards. These findings also confirm the absence of social desirability
   261в†’   285в†’   285в†’and demand effects observed in our previous study. Further investigations are needed to
   262в†’   286в†’   286в†’determine whether these effects of an initial craving measure are beneficial or represent a
   263в†’   287в†’   287в†’significant bias in craving studies, and why they may not apply to women.
