   768→    "description": "Calderon and her colleagues (2026) failed to replicate Constural Level Theory’s (CLT, Liberman &amp; Trope, 2014; Trope &amp; Liberman, 2010) finding that psychologically more distant actions—actions that are planned for the more distant future, for more distant places, for less likely situations and those performed by more socially distant persons- are construed more abstractly. There is considerable evidence for the effect of psychological distance on construal level, including recent, well-powered, preregistered studies. There is also a theory behind this effect: Higher-level constructs change less over distance. So why did the replication fail? What can we learn from it about (studying) distance and level-of-construal, and more generally, about multi-lab replications? This commentary suggests that in several respects, the replication project fell short of meeting established standards of psychological research. The results of the project are partly based on samples that included many inattentive participants, on responders who were not native speakers of the language in which the experiment was presented, on translations that did not preserve the original intent of the materials, on manipulations of distance that were confusing, weak, or confounded with valence and on manipulation-checks that did not check the actual manipulations. The commentary also offers a re-analysis of some of Calderon and colleagues’ (2026) data and a new replication study, both of which show an effect of temporal distance on construal level. Finally, we suggest two ways in which the Registered Replication Report format may be improved.",
