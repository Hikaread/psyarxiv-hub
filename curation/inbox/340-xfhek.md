## Summary

This co-produced qualitative study explored the parenting experiences of 19 Autistic fathers using semi-structured interviews (spoken or written, per participant preference), analyzed with reflexive thematic analysis. Four Autistic fathers collaborated as co-researchers in study design, interview development, and analysis. Five themes were generated: finding joy and meaning in everyday fatherhood moments; becoming oneself through fatherhood, where parenting strengthened Autistic identity; walking the tightrope of family life, reflecting the continuous effort of balancing sensory, emotional, and practical demands; parenting as relational work, including the tension arising when fathers felt misunderstood by partners and family; and parenting in systems stacked against us, highlighting barriers in services and wider society that fail to recognize Autistic needs.

## Clinical Insight

Autistic fathers in this study described fatherhood as a source of identity consolidation and emotional connection, challenging the deficit-focused narrative that still predominates in clinical work with neurodivergent adults. The theme of walking a tightrope of sensory, emotional, and practical demands highlights that autistic parents may experience parenthood not as a category of impairment but as an ongoing balancing act that can become overwhelming when environmental demands exceed capacity. The finding that fathers felt most distressed not by parenting itself but by being misunderstood within family relationships and by services suggests that clinicians working with neurodivergent families should attend to the relational context and systemic barriers rather than focusing solely on individual coping strategies. When an autistic father presents in therapy, asking about parenting experiences may open a productive avenue for exploring identity, self-worth, and systemic stressors.

## Relevant For

Therapists working with neurodivergent adults, particularly autistic parents or those navigating family life and relationship dynamics
