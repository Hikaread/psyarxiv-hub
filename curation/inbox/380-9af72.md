## Summary

This study uses a network approach to examine associations between social cognition measures and alcohol use severity, exploring how these relationships differ across age and sex. Rather than studying social processes in isolation, the network framework maps concurrent relationships between multiple social cognitive factors and alcohol outcomes, providing a more ecologically valid picture of how social cognition intersects with drinking behavior in different demographic groups.

## Clinical Insight

Understanding how social cognitive processes interact with alcohol use helps therapists move beyond simple cause-effect models. The network approach reveals which social cognitive factors are most centrally connected to alcohol use severity, potentially identifying priority targets for intervention. The age and sex differences suggest that social cognitive interventions for alcohol use disorder may need to be tailored differently across demographic groups, informing personalized treatment planning.

## Relevant For

Addiction therapists, clinicians treating alcohol use disorder, therapists incorporating social cognition into addiction treatment, therapists working with diverse demographic groups.

## Notes

Single author limits perspective. Network approach is well-suited for exploring complex associations. Age and sex stratification adds clinical utility.
