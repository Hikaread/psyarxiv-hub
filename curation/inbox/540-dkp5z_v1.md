## Summary
This **mixed-methods international study** investigates the extent and nature of **algorithmic exposure to body image content** on TikTok, its psychological impacts, and users' coping strategies. TikTok's **recommendation algorithm** curates personalized feeds that can expose individuals to a spectrum of body image content ranging from body acceptance material to content promoting **extreme thinness**, fitness ideals, and **pro-eating disorder (pro-ED) messaging**. The study examines exposure patterns across diverse international participants, assesses psychological impacts including **body dissatisfaction** and disordered eating cognitions, and identifies the **coping strategies** users employ. The research aims to generate actionable implications for **digital public health interventions**, platform accountability, and policy. This is particularly relevant given TikTok's billions of global users and the platform's role as a rapidly evolving frontier for public mental health.

## Clinical Insight
For clinicians working with eating disorders and body image concerns, this study provides crucial evidence about how **algorithmic curation** on TikTok may systematically expose vulnerable users to harmful body image content, including pro-ED messaging. Understanding the specific mechanisms by which recommendation algorithms create **"filter bubbles"** of body-focused content helps clinicians assess and address **digital environmental risk factors** in treatment. The identification of user coping strategies offers potential **therapeutic tools** that clinicians can recommend or build upon. The study also strengthens the case for **psychoeducation about algorithmic content curation** as part of treatment, particularly for younger clients who may not understand how recommendation systems shape their content exposure. Clinicians should consider **routine assessment of social media algorithm exposure patterns** as part of their formulation for patients with eating disorders or body dysmorphia.
