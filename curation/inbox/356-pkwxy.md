## Summary

This study developed and psychometrically evaluated a German Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA) item set for assessing psychological chronic pain processes. Across three phases—pilot testing (3 people with chronic pain), main evaluation (n=68, 6 assessments/day for 12 days, 4,236 datapoints), and preliminary re-evaluation (n=10, 6 assessments/day for 21 days)—the researchers examined variability and reliability of items capturing clinically relevant pain processes. Most items and process scales met predefined criteria, though four of thirteen scales showed insufficient reliability. Variance was largely attributable to between-person differences. The study presents the first psychometrically evaluated German EMA item set for chronic pain processes.

## Clinical Insight

This paper offers therapists working with chronic pain patients a validated tool for tracking pain processes in real-time between sessions. EMA-based assessment can capture within-person fluctuations that retrospective questionnaires miss, enabling more personalized treatment monitoring. Therapists can use insights about day-level patterns (e.g., catastrophizing, pain-related fear, disability) to tailor interventions and track progress more precisely. The finding that variance is mostly between-person underscores the importance of individualized rather than one-size-fits-all approaches to chronic pain treatment.

## Relevant For

- Pain psychologists and therapists working with chronic pain populations
- Clinicians using EMA or experience sampling methods for monitoring treatment progress
- Researchers developing assessment tools for chronic pain
- Clinical psychologists interested in within-person process measurement
