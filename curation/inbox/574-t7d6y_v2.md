## Summary

This **pragmatic review** addresses the decline in **experimenter training** within **experimental psychopathology (EPP)** and its consequences for research fidelity and replicability. A systematic search identified only **seven articles** providing methodological guidance for laboratory-based EPP paradigms, with just one offering an explicit **experimenter training framework**. Using the **trauma film paradigm (TFP)** as a worked example — a widely used experimental model for studying **intrusive memories** and **PTSD-relevant processes** — the authors integrate insights from the reviewed literature with approaches from **adjacent clinical trials** to propose a comprehensive framework for experimenter training in EPP. The paper argues that insufficient training increases **experimenter noise** and **protocol drift**, potentially hindering the translation of laboratory findings to clinical intervention development. The proposed framework addresses both the **technical/procedural** and **interpersonal** dimensions of experimenter competence, with direct implications for the replicability of EPP studies that inform treatments for trauma and anxiety disorders. ## Clinical Insight

While this paper targets researchers rather than clinicians directly, its implications for **evidence-based practice** are significant. The **trauma film paradigm** has been instrumental in developing **imagery-based interventions** for PTSD, including **imagery rescripting** and **competitive memory training**. If the paradigm produces unreliable results due to experimenter variability, the clinical interventions derived from it may be built on an unstable evidence base. For **clinical psychologists** who follow the research-to-practice pipeline, this paper highlights a often-overlooked threat to evidence quality. The proposed training framework — emphasising standardised interpersonal interactions, fidelity monitoring, and clear protocol documentation — mirrors best practices in **clinical trial integrity** that therapists already value. For **training directors** in clinical psychology doctoral programmes, the paper's framework could be adapted for training research assistants and junior clinicians in delivering experimental and therapeutic protocols with high fidelity. The emphasis on the interpersonal dimension of experimenter behaviour (tone, warmth, standardised instructions) directly parallels therapist factors known to affect **therapeutic alliance** and treatment outcomes.

## Relevant For
- Clinical researchers using experimental paradigms to study trauma and anxiety
- Training directors in clinical psychology programmes
- Research coordinators managing EPP or clinical trial fidelity
- Clinicians interested in the evidence base underlying imagery-based trauma treatments
