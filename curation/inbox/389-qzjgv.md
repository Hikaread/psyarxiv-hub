## Summary
This two-study paper develops a questionnaire battery to assess workplace well-being among workers with intellectual disabilities in sheltered employment. Study 1 involved content validation by an expert committee using the Lynn method for all 80 items across four well-being indicators (burnout, need for recovery, vigor, job satisfaction), job characteristics, and off-duty recovery. Study 2 administered the validated questionnaire to 29 participants with intellectual disabilities to assess accessibility. The instrument is grounded in the Job Demands-Resources model, the DISC-R model, and recovery theories.

## Clinical Insight
Therapists supporting adults with intellectual disabilities often navigate vocational challenges without adequate assessment tools. This instrument fills a gap by providing a structured way to evaluate the interplay between job demands, resources, and recovery in this population. Clinicians can use it to identify specific workplace stressors and strengths, informing targeted interventions such as job carving, workplace accommodations, or stress management training. The focus on off-duty recovery is particularly relevant, as many individuals with intellectual disabilities may have limited capacity for self-directed leisure activities that promote recovery.

## Relevant For
Therapists in intellectual disability services, vocational rehabilitation, supported employment programs, and occupational health.

## Notes
Pilot sample of 29 is very small for psychometric validation. Further validation with larger samples is needed. Focused on sheltered employment settings, which may not generalise to open employment.
