## Summary
This paper introduces **ZolaBongo**, an open-source Android tablet application that enables **offline cognitive testing** using experiments built in **PsychoPy** and hosted on **Pavlovia**. The tool was developed to address the challenge of conducting cognitive assessments in **understudied populations** who lack reliable internet access. ZolaBongo was validated with children (age 4+, N = 170) and adults (N = 178) at two sites: the **Democratic Republic of the Congo** (DRC) and **South Africa**. The cognitive task battery measured **reasoning**, **executive function**, and **working memory** — tasks selected by the **African Brain and Cognitive Development Network (AfriBCD)**, a community of practice spanning 17 African countries. Results demonstrated that the tablet-based tasks worked effectively in both populations, with participants including those with little to no prior technology exposure completing the tasks successfully. The app downloads Pavlovia experiments for offline use, stores data locally, and syncs to Pavlovia's secure cloud storage when internet becomes available.

## Clinical Insight
ZolaBongo has significant implications for **clinical assessment** in low-resource and conflict-affected settings where traditional cognitive testing is impractical. In the DRC site (North Kivu province), where approximately 8.4 million civilians are internally displaced and fieldwork must be conducted in community settings without lab infrastructure, this tool enables **trauma-related cognitive assessments** that would otherwise be impossible. For clinicians and researchers working with **refugee populations**, **underserved communities in majority-world countries**, or **rural settings** without reliable internet, ZolaBongo provides a pathway to culturally adaptable cognitive testing. The ability to create experiments using PsychoPy's GUI (no programming required) and deploy them on low-cost Android tablets dramatically lowers the barrier for clinical researchers who need to adapt measures for **cross-cultural cognitive assessment**. The open-source nature means tasks can be translated and modified for local contexts, addressing a critical gap in culturally valid neuropsychological assessment. The current validation focused on non-clinical cognitive tasks; future work extending to clinical measures (e.g., trauma-related cognition, executive dysfunction in psychopathology) would enhance its direct clinical utility.

## Methodology Note
__Measurement Validity__: The cognitive tasks (reasoning, executive function, working memory) were selected by the AfriBCD network and validated across two distinct African populations (DRC and South Africa); however, the paper focuses primarily on demonstrating feasibility rather than establishing full psychometric properties (reliability, validity, measurement invariance) of the specific tasks within these populations. __Open Science__: ZolaBongo itself is open-source, and the paper is open access on PsyArXiv; the tool is designed to integrate with the existing PsychoPy/Pavlovia open-source ecosystem, and the development team includes Open Science Tools Ltd. __Participant Transparency__: The study tested children (age 4+, N = 170) and adults (N = 178) at two sites in the DRC and South Africa; the DRC sample included participants from conflict-affected North Kivu province, and the South Africa sample included children from low-income urban and rural settings in the Cape Town area; specific demographic breakdowns (gender, exact age distributions, languages) were reported in the full paper. This is an other-type paper (software/tool development paper), so Statistical Power, Preregistration, and Effect Sizes dimensions are not applicable.
