## Summary
This ALE meta-analysis aggregated fMRI findings (35 studies; 261 ASC, 289 NT participants) examining prediction and prediction error processing in autism spectrum condition. NT > ASC activation differences converged in a single 744 mm³ cluster in the medial frontal gyrus/cingulate gyrus (BA 9/32), overlapping the dorsal anterior cingulate cortex, a region implicated in error monitoring and uncertainty estimation. ASC > NT contrasts (8 experiments, 139 ASC, 150 NT) showed no significant convergence. Notably, 58.73% of all extracted experiments were null results, and a Fail-Safe N analysis indicated low robustness of the sole significant cluster, suggesting the finding should be interpreted cautiously.

## Clinical Insight
The reduced dACC response to prediction violation in autism may reflect attenuated differentiation between expected and unexpected outcomes, which could inform interventions targeting adaptive learning and cognitive flexibility. However, the high null rate means these neural findings are not yet ready to guide individualized clinical strategies—computational modeling studies are needed first to specify underlying mechanisms.

## Relevant For
Neuropsychologists and researchers studying predictive processing in autism

## Notes
Severely limited by small experiment counts (maximum 13 merged for NT > ASC; simulations recommend ≥17–20), heterogeneous age groups, and possible thresholding differences between significant and null studies that may have introduced systematic bias.
