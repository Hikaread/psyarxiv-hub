## Summary

This study develops and validates the Smoking Exposure Application (SEA), an augmented reality (AR) tool for smoking cue-exposure therapy. By bridging laboratory cue-reactivity paradigms with real-world environments, SEA allows clients to encounter smoking cues in ecologically valid settings through their smartphone. The study reports on the app development, validation of cue materials, and preliminary testing of its ability to elicit craving responses relevant to smoking cessation treatment.

## Clinical Insight

Cue-exposure therapy is an established component of addiction treatment, but its effectiveness is limited by the gap between controlled clinic environments and the real-world contexts where cravings occur. AR technology offers therapists a practical tool to extend cue-exposure work beyond the therapy room, enabling clients to practice craving management in settings they actually encounter in daily life. Therapists working in smoking cessation can integrate this as a between-session homework tool, enhancing the ecological validity of exposure-based interventions and potentially improving treatment outcomes.

## Relevant For

Addiction therapists, smoking cessation specialists, therapists using cue-exposure approaches, clinicians integrating digital tools into addiction treatment.

## Notes

AR approach is innovative but still in early validation stages. Focuses on smoking cues specifically. May have applications for other substance cue exposures.
