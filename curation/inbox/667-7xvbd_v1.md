## Summary
Two experiments (total N = 191) investigated whether **deficient visuospatial orienting** during encoding and maintenance underlies **working memory (WM) deficits in adults with ADHD symptoms**. Experiment 1 used a **colour recognition task** with pre-cues (orienting attention before encoding) and retro-cues (orienting internal attention during maintenance) in **84 symptomatic** and **107 control** participants. Experiment 2 replicated the design using **unfamiliar shapes** to minimize verbal rehearsal. Across both experiments, **strong pre- and retro-cueing effects** on accuracy and reaction time confirmed that the paradigm engages attentional orienting mechanisms. However, **no group differences** in cueing benefits emerged, and **no correlations** between ADHD symptom scores (Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale) and cueing effects were found. These results converge with previous findings that **reward-based prioritization** is also preserved in adult ADHD, suggesting that **core attentional control mechanisms in WM are intact** in this population. The authors propose that real-world WM difficulties in ADHD may instead reflect deficits in **intentional disengagement** from irrelevant information in continuous, interference-rich contexts.

## Clinical Insight
For clinicians assessing and treating **adult ADHD**, these findings challenge the common assumption that **working memory deficits** in ADHD reflect impaired attentional control. The preserved ability to use both **pre-cues** (perceptual attention) and **retro-cues** (internal attention) suggests that adults with ADHD can **strategically prioritize information** in WM when the task structure supports it. This has practical implications for **psychoeducation and compensatory strategy training**: rather than trying to improve a supposedly impaired attentional mechanism, interventions should focus on helping patients **create external structure** (e.g., explicit prioritization cues, organized environments, task chunking) that leverages their intact attentional orienting abilities. The authors' proposal that the core deficit may lie in **intentional disengagement** from irrelevant mental representations aligns clinically with patients' reports of being **easily distracted by internal thoughts** rather than external stimuli. This suggests that **mindfulness-based approaches** targeting meta-awareness of internal distractions may be more relevant than attentional training programs. The study also supports the use of **cueing strategies** in occupational and educational settings for adults with ADHD — providing clear external signals about what to prioritize appears to engage intact neurocognitive mechanisms.

## Methodology Note
This is a quantitative_experimental study.

__Statistical Power__: The total sample of N = 191 (84 ADHD-symptomatic, 107 controls) provides adequate power for detecting medium effects.
The study was designed to extend prior underpowered work in this area.
Bayesian analyses were conducted to complement frequentist tests, providing evidence for the null hypothesis.

__Preregistration__: The study materials and data are available on OSF.
No explicit mention of preregistration, though the open materials support transparency.
The confirmatory hypothesis testing approach with Bayesian complements is methodologically sound.

__Effect Sizes__: Bayesian t-test Bayes factors are reported, providing evidence for or against the null hypothesis.
The abstract does not report specific effect sizes for the null results, though these are likely detailed in the full text.
The use of Bayes factors rather than only p-values is appropriate for evaluating null findings.

__Statistical Rigor__: Two experiments with different stimulus types (colours vs. shapes) strengthen the internal validity.
The ADHD symptomatic group was defined using the Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale with established cut-offs.
Both frequentist and Bayesian analyses were conducted, providing converging evidence.

__Measurement Validity__: ADHD symptoms were assessed using the well-validated ASRS.
The experimental paradigm (pre- and retro-cueing) is established in the WM literature.
The use of unfamiliar shapes in Experiment 2 to control for verbal rehearsal is a methodological strength.

__Open Science__: Materials and data are openly available on OSF.
This supports reproducibility and allows independent verification of the findings.
The open data approach is commendable for a null-results study.

__Participant Transparency__: Adults with a mean age of ~29 years were recruited, with clear inclusion criteria for the symptomatic group.
Both experiments included gender-balanced samples.
The community-based recruitment (rather than clinical referral) may affect the generalizability to diagnosed populations.
