## Summary

This research develops and validates a picture-story measure of the need for sex using an experimental-manipulation, measure, indicator, and criterion (EMMIC) validation approach. The resulting coding system includes a measure of sexual conflict alongside the need for sex, with reliably coded categories that capture individual differences in sexual motivation. The measure is designed to assess an implicit motivational need rather than self-reported sexual behavior or attitudes.

## Clinical Insight

Sex therapists frequently encounter discrepancies between clients reported sexual attitudes and their underlying motivational dynamics. A validated measure of the need for sex as a motivational construct (distinct from sexual behavior or satisfaction) gives therapists a more nuanced tool for assessing sexual dynamics in couple therapy. The sexual conflict component is particularly relevant for couples presenting with sexual desire discrepancy, where understanding the underlying motivational needs can guide more effective intervention than focusing solely on behavioral frequency or satisfaction scores.

## Relevant For

Sex therapists, couples therapists, therapists working with sexual desire discrepancy, clinicians using motivational approaches in sexology.

## Notes

EMMIC validation approach is rigorous. Picture-story method captures implicit motivation. Sexual conflict measure adds clinical utility beyond simple need assessment.
