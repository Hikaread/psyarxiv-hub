## Summary

This registered report (N=595 Hungarian online gamblers) experimentally tested whether the availability of a cash-out feature in online sports betting causally influences gambling behavior. Participants were randomized to conditions where cash-out was either available or unavailable. Contrary to correlational literature suggesting cash-outs intensify problematic gambling, the experiment found no effect of cash-out availability on bet placement, wager size, or bet risk. However, the authors note that low variance in outcomes may have limited detection of subtle effects, so the null results should not be taken as conclusive evidence of no behavioral impact.

## Clinical Insight

For therapists treating gambling disorder, this study provides important experimental evidence that challenges the assumption that cash-out features directly drive problematic betting. While correlational studies have linked cash-outs to harm, this causal test did not replicate that finding. Clinicians should be cautious about attributing clients' gambling problems primarily to specific platform features and instead focus on broader psychological mechanisms. The study also illustrates how correlational and experimental evidence can diverge, a useful psychoeducation point when discussing gambling behavior with clients who may blame the platform rather than their own patterns.

## Relevant For

- Therapists treating gambling disorder and behavioral addictions
- Addiction counselors working with online gambling issues
- Clinicians interested in evidence-based psychoeducation about gambling mechanisms
- Researchers studying gambling behavior and policy
