## Summary
This methodological paper describes in detail how to implement a fully crossed stratified sampling design using the Prolific platform to ensure adequate representation across three key demographic dimensions: race/ethnicity, gender, and socioeconomic status. The study targets 1,008 U.S. adults examining fear of happiness and depression. The authors discuss practical challenges encountered during recruitment, including uncategorizable cases and the need for SES-based recruitment blocks, and provide recommendations for maintaining analytic rigor while pursuing equity in sampling.

## Clinical Insight
Evidence-based practice requires that the evidence base itself is representative of the clinical population. This paper's methodology is directly relevant to therapists because it demonstrates how research can move beyond WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) samples. The focus on fear of happiness, an emotion relevant to many clinical presentations including depression and complex trauma, underscores how demographic variation in emotional experience may affect treatment planning and expected outcomes.

## Relevant For
Clinician-researchers designing therapy outcome studies, therapists interested in cultural competence and research literacy, training program directors.

## Notes
Excluded non-cisgender identities from the stratification design, which is a limitation for full LGBTQ+ representation. SES defined by household income brackets specific to the U.S. context.
