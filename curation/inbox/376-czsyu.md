## Summary

This participatory co-design study develops a co-creation process for building shared causal frameworks about psychosocial processes in chronic pain. Using facilitated workshops with multiple stakeholders including people with lived pain experience, clinicians, and researchers, the study produces a shared causal diagram that identifies meaningful research questions and guides data modeling decisions. The approach bridges the gap between clinical experience and research evidence.

## Clinical Insight

Therapists working with chronic pain clients often observe complex psychosocial interactions that are difficult to capture in standardized assessments. This co-created causal modeling approach offers a structured method for therapists to articulate their clinical observations about how psychological, social, and pain-related factors interact in individual cases. The participatory framework also models how therapists and clients can collaborate to develop shared understanding of pain processes, which has direct application to psychoeducation and collaborative case formulation in pain management.

## Relevant For

Pain psychologists, chronic pain rehabilitation therapists, therapists using collaborative formulation approaches, clinicians working in multidisciplinary pain teams.

## Notes

Participatory approach with lived experience involvement is a significant strength. Focus on chronic pain psychosocial processes is highly relevant for clinical practice. Method is transferable to other complex clinical presentations.
