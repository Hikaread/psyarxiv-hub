## Summary
This paper presents the **Depressometer Project**, a multimodal intensive longitudinal dataset capturing the mechanisms of change in **16-session supportive-expressive psychodynamic psychotherapy** for **Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)**. The dataset includes **58 patients** (21–61 years) and **13 therapists**, with **816 sessions** recorded across six microphones and three cameras. Data were collected at multiple temporal resolutions: treatment-level, session-level, and follow-up self-reports, plus within-session **linguistic, vocal, facial, movement, physiological, and hormonal measures**. A subsample was manually coded for patient affective/behavioral/cognitive experiences and therapist interventions. The corpus includes approximately **six million words** of transcribed therapy dialogue. Recovery rates were notable: **68.6%** of patients who began above the HRSD clinical cutoff ended below it, and **67.9%** showed reliable BDI improvement.

## Clinical Insight
This dataset represents a uniquely rich resource for **psychotherapy process research**. For clinicians, the multimodal design demonstrates that therapeutic change unfolds through **interconnected vocal, facial, linguistic, physiological, and relational processes** — not just self-reported symptoms. The 68.6% recovery rate on the HRSD is clinically meaningful for brief psychodynamic therapy. The within-session granularity (word-level transcripts, physiological markers) enables investigation of specific **in-session events** that predict recovery, such as therapist interventions that shift patient emotional arousal. The human annotations designed to support LLM-based analysis also signal a new frontier for **computational psychotherapy research**. Clinicians interested in **psychodynamic change mechanisms** or **process-outcome research** will find this dataset particularly valuable.

## Methodology Note
This is a dataset/descriptive methodology paper (no original hypothesis testing; describes an intensive longitudinal psychotherapy dataset).

__Design__: Naturalistic outpatient psychotherapy study with 58 MDD patients and 13 therapists in a university clinic setting.
16-session supportive-expressive psychodynamic therapy (Luborsky model) with extensive multimodal data collection.
Treatment delivered by advanced trainees (3–7 years experience) with weekly individual and group supervision.
Six patients dropped out before session 12 and were excluded, yielding a completer sample of 58.

__Measurement Validity__: MDD diagnosis confirmed via MINI 5.0 structured clinical interview.
Depression severity measured with HRSD (clinician-rated), BDI-II (self-report), OQ-45, and HSCL.
Multiple additional measures capture interpersonal functioning, therapeutic alliance, and session quality.
The multimodal approach (linguistic, vocal, facial, physiological, hormonal) provides convergent measurement of within-session processes.

__Open Science__: Published as an open-access preprint on PsyArXiv.
The paper is a detailed method documentation designed to support secondary analysis of the dataset.
No mention of whether the raw data are publicly available for external researchers.

__Participant Transparency__: 64 patients initially enrolled; 6 dropped out before session 12.
Final N = 58 (37 women, 21 men; M age 36.66, SD 9.45).
Majority Israeli-born (42/58), 44 native Hebrew speakers.
Inclusion: primary MDD diagnosis, HRSD >= 14 or BDI-II >= 17.
Exclusion: active suicidality, psychosis, addictions, bipolar disorder, brain damage, pregnancy.
13 therapists (7 women, 6 men; M age 32.2, range 28–41; 3–7 years experience).
