## Summary
In a cross-sectional survey of 291 U.S. animal care workers across six sectors, McNally examined whether 12 specific occupational exposures differentially predicted moral injury (MIOS; α = .91), secondary traumatic stress (ProQOL-5 STS; α = .86), burnout (ProQOL-5; α = .73), and depression (PHQ-9; α = .89). Total exposure dose predicted STS (r = .22), burnout (r = .13), and depression (r = .14) but not moral injury (r = .06, p = .30), demonstrating that moral injury is driven by exposure type rather than cumulative dose. Euthanasia decision-making and administration predicted STS and burnout but not moral injury, whereas handling deceased animal remains uniquely predicted moral injury total (β = .188, p = .022) and trust violations (β = .202, p = .015) after controlling for both euthanasia variables. Public threats and moral accusations was the only exposure predicting all four conditions and added significant incremental variance for moral injury (ΔR² = .039, p = .012). Clinical moral injury (17.4% of sample) was characterized by elevated interpersonal threat exposure, not by elevated animal care task exposure.

## Clinical Insight
Clinicians working with animal care workers should assess for moral injury separately from burnout and STS rather than treating these as a single compassion fatigue construct, since each has a distinct exposure profile. Interventions targeting moral injury need to address the moral-identity threat from public accusations and the institutional betrayal inherent in how remains are handled, rather than focusing primarily on euthanasia-related distress, which appears to drive STS and burnout but not moral injury.

## Relevant For
Clinicians and organizational consultants supporting animal care workers, shelter medicine professionals, and those working with moral injury in non-military populations.

## Notes
Cross-sectional design with a convenience sample limits causal inference. The 12-item exposure checklist is author-constructed and not yet validated independently, and data availability is restricted.
