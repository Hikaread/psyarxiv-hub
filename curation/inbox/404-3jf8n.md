## Summary
Using data from 3,835 youth in the Healthy Brain Network (mean age = 10.25 years; 63% male), the authors applied the Graded Response Model and computerized adaptive testing (CAT) simulations to reduce the 30-item E-SWAN DMDD questionnaire to a 4-item short form. The short form correlated highly with the full form (Levy-corrected r = 0.776) and showed moderate convergent validity with the Affective Reactivity Index (ρ = 0.231) and CBCL/TRF Externalizing domains (ρ = 0.345–0.394). Discriminant validity evidence was strong: the short form had a significantly weaker association with the general psychopathology p-factor than the full form (ρ = 0.424 vs. 0.530, p < .001), suggesting greater specificity for DMDD. Convergent validity with the ARI was replicated in an independent University of Pennsylvania sample (ρ = 0.307, p < .001).

## Clinical Insight
The 4-item E-SWAN DMDD short form offers a practical, brief self-report screener for DMDD-specific irritability that reduces response burden in children and adolescents without sacrificing construct validity. Its stronger specificity for DMDD over general psychopathology makes it particularly useful for distinguishing DMDD from comorbid conditions in triage and screening settings.

## Relevant For
Clinicians and researchers assessing irritability and mood dysregulation in youth ages 6–18.

## Notes
Short–full form correlations were derived from the same administration sitting despite Levy correction; multi-informant (parent-report) validation is still needed. The sample was drawn from a community-based research initiative and may not generalize to clinical inpatient populations.
