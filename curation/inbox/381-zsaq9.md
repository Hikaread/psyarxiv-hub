## Summary

This article provides the first comprehensive review of obsessive thinking about a loved one in romantic love, organizing the literature using an Eight Perspectives approach spanning evolutionary, clinical, cognitive, neurobiological, developmental, cross-cultural, comparative, and applied dimensions. The review examines the boundaries between normative romantic obsession, obsessive-compulsive patterns, and clinical pathology.

## Clinical Insight

Therapists frequently encounter clients whose relationship preoccupation blurs the line between intense romantic attachment and clinically significant obsessive thinking. This review provides a multi-perspective framework for distinguishing normative romantic obsession from OCD-spectrum pathology, which is directly applicable to clinical assessment. Understanding the evolutionary and neurobiological underpinnings of romantic obsession helps therapists normalize certain experiences while identifying when intrusive thoughts about a partner cross into clinically significant territory requiring intervention.

## Relevant For

Therapists working with relationship distress, OCD specialists seeing clients with relationship-focused obsessions, couples therapists, clinicians assessing obsessive-compulsive patterns in romantic relationships.
