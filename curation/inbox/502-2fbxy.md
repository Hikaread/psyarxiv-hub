## Summary

The **Depressometer** project is a comprehensive method paper describing a **multimodal longitudinal dataset** for studying mechanisms of change in **psychotherapy for depression**. Data were collected from **58 patients** diagnosed with **Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)** and **13 therapists** during 16-session **supportive-expressive (SE) psychodynamic psychotherapy** at Bar-Ilan University. The dataset spans treatment-level, session-level, and within-session temporal resolutions, integrating self-report, clinician-rated, linguistic, vocal, facial, movement, physiological, and hormonal measures across **816 recorded sessions**.

The final cohort included 58 patients (M = 36.66 years, SD = 9.45; 64% female), diagnosed via the **MINI 5.0** and meeting entry criteria of **HRSD ≥ 14** or **BDI-II ≥ 17**. Treatment change indices showed that **68.6%** of patients scoring above the HRSD clinical cutoff at intake ended treatment below it, while **67.4%** showed reliable **BDI-II** improvement (RCI = 5.78) and **54.3%** showed reliable **ORS** improvement. The corpus includes approximately **six million words** of transcribed therapeutic dialogue (average 7,758 words per session), translated from Hebrew to English.

Multiple coding systems were applied to the "working phase" (mid-session) of five sessions per patient: the **MIND framework** (Multimodal Interpersonal and Intrapersonal Dynamics), coding ABCD self-state elements (Affect, Behavior, Cognition, Desire) at speech-turn and 5-minute segment levels; the **CIB** (Coding Interactive Behavioral Manual) for dyadic synchrony and tension (ICC = 0.76); **CEAS-III** for client emotional arousal (average ICC = .74); and **PIC** for therapist interventions. An **LLM-based framework** with four specialized agents (Supportive, Meaning-making, Exploratory, Directive) and a Judge-LLM was developed to compare AI-generated versus human therapist interventions. Physiological measures included **ECG** (for HRV), **EDA**, and **oxytocin** (salivary, pg/ml) collected across five sessions. Behavioral measures used **FaceReader-9** for facial expression valence and **Motion Energy Analysis (MEA)** for body movement.

## Clinical Insight

The Depressometer dataset offers an unprecedented resource for understanding **how change unfolds within and across psychotherapy sessions** for depression. Clinicians may find this dataset particularly valuable because it operationalizes the **MIND framework**, which breaks down patient experiences into **core experiential building blocks (ABCD)** and maps how therapists' interventions interact with these shifting self-states moment-by-moment. The finding that **67.9%** of patients showed reliable BDI-II improvement and **52.7%** dropped below the HRSD clinical cutoff supports the efficacy of brief **SE psychodynamic therapy** in a naturalistic training clinic setting.

The multimodal design—integrating **verbal, vocal, facial, physiological, and hormonal data**—enables investigation of questions that single-modality studies cannot address, such as whether **dyadic synchrony** between patient and therapist predicts session-level outcomes, or how **oxytocin** responses during sessions relate to therapeutic alliance. The LLM component comparing AI-generated versus human interventions raises timely questions about the future role of **computational models in psychotherapy research** and training.

## Methodology Note

This is an other-type paper (detailed methodology/dataset description paper). Statistical Power, Preregistration, and Effect Sizes are not applicable.

__Statistical Rigor__: The paper provides a thorough description of the multimodal dataset and coding procedures.
Treatment change indices were calculated using **reliable change indices (RCI)** with clinically meaningful cutoffs for the **HRSD** (cutoff = 14), **BDI-II** (cutoff = 17), **ORS** (cutoff = 24), and **HSCL-11** (cutoff = 1.92).
Inter-annotator agreement for the MIND framework was evaluated using **Gwet's AC1/AC2** coefficients alongside Krippendorff's α, with AC2 values ranging from moderate (.48) to near-ceiling (1.00) across dimensions.
The study acknowledges the **kappa paradox** and appropriately treats AC1/AC2 as primary coefficients given the low base rates of many psychotherapy markers.
CIB coding showed good inter-rater reliability (ICC = 0.76, range = 0.57–0.87).
CEAS-III emotional arousal coding achieved average ICC = .74 with 90% agreement on emotional valence.
LLM versus human intervention evaluation used three blinded expert judges.

__Measurement Validity__: Measures included well-validated instruments: **HRSD** (clinician-administered), **BDI-II** (α = .93), **OQ-45** (subscales α = .71–.91), **DERS** (α = .80–.93), **WAI-SR** (α = .85–.95), **ERS** (α = .86), **SES** (α = .84), **IIP-32**, **SWLS** (α = .87–.91), **PID-5-BF**, **RSE** (α = .93), **SDS**, **MULTI-30**, **POMS**, and **HSCL-11** (α = .92).
Physiological measures (ECG, EDA) used **Mindware software** with validated artifact-editing algorithms.
Facial expression analysis used **FaceReader-9** based on the **Facial Action Coding System (FACS)**, sampling at 25 fps.
Oxytocin was assayed in duplicates using auto-analyzer EVO75 with internal quality controls.
Hebrew transcripts were translated to English using Google Cloud Translation API, which introduces a potential source of measurement error.

__Open Science__: This is a method/dataset paper describing a resource for future research.
The paper does not report whether data, code, or materials are openly available beyond the method description.
No preregistration is mentioned.
The dataset appears to be an internal research resource at Bar-Ilan University's Psychotherapy Research Lab.

__Participant Transparency__: Sample of 58 patients with MDD (from 64 enrolled; 6 dropouts removed) and 13 therapists at a university clinic in Israel.
Patients were predominantly female (64%), Israeli-born (72%), and native Hebrew speakers (76%).
Mean age was 36.66 years (SD = 9.45, range 21–61).
Inclusion required primary MDD diagnosis via MINI 5.0 and HRSD ≥ 14 or BDI-II ≥ 17.
Exclusions: active suicidality, psychosis, addictions, bipolar disorder, brain damage, pregnancy, heart problems.
Therapists were advanced trainees (3–7 years experience; M age = 32.2 years).
Only 3 patients took psychiatric medication during treatment.
The sample is relatively homogeneous (Israeli, largely Hebrew-speaking, university-affiliated clinic), limiting generalizability.

## Notes

This is a method paper, not a results paper—it describes the Depressometer dataset infrastructure. Multiple published and forthcoming studies analyze different aspects of this dataset (e.g., Paz et al., 2021, 2024, 2026; Sayda et al., 2026; Stolowicz-Melman et al., 2026). The LLM framework comparing AI-generated versus human interventions represents a novel methodological contribution at the intersection of psychotherapy research and artificial intelligence.
