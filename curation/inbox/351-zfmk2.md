## Summary

This qualitative study examined how 15 adolescents (mean age 16.74 years) integrated mindfulness into daily life one month after a six-day immersive mindfulness retreat. Using three-cycle coding, the study identified intra-personal applications (self-awareness and regulation reported by 100%, self-compassion and acceptance by 73%, broader perspective by 60%) and inter-personal applications (navigating social anxiety and building connections by 73%, compassionate communication by 67%, social awareness and attunement by 53%). All adolescents described experiences in both domains, suggesting the retreat fostered transferable skills across personal and social contexts. The immersive format contrasted with typical brief school-based programs.

## Clinical Insight

This study is valuable for therapists who recommend mindfulness to adolescent clients because it provides concrete examples of how young people actually use these skills, rather than assuming transfer from program completion. The finding that all adolescents applied mindfulness both intra-personally and inter-personally challenges the common therapeutic focus on mindfulness as a purely internal regulation tool. For therapists working with socially anxious teens, the finding that 73% used mindfulness to navigate social situations — including reducing social apprehension and engaging in active listening — suggests that mindfulness can be framed specifically as a social skill, which may increase engagement for adolescents who are ambivalent about introspective practices. The retreat format showing strong transfer at one month also raises the question of whether intensity matters more than duration: a six-day immersive experience may consolidate skills more effectively than weeks of brief sessions, which has implications for how therapists structure mindfulness homework and skills practice between sessions.

## Relevant For

Therapists incorporating mindfulness into work with adolescents, particularly those presenting with social anxiety or interpersonal difficulties
