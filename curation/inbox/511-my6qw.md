## Summary

This **ecological momentary intervention** study tested whether training individuals to use **wise empathy** on Instagram could improve well-being. Half the sample (N = 260) was trained to wisely regulate empathy for positive and negative posts, while the other half served as controls. Using **experience sampling** (N observations = 5,902), empathy and well-being were tracked in daily life. The intervention increased **wise empathy online**—boosting **appreciative joy**, **positive emotion sharing**, and **compassion** for negative emotions—while reducing **negative emotion sharing**, **upward social comparisons**, and **personal distress**. When participants had recently browsed Instagram, those in the wise empathy condition reported **higher well-being** than controls, an effect **mediated by changes in wise empathy**. These findings position wise empathy as a **scalable, trainable skill** that can enhance well-being during social media use.

## Clinical Insight

For clinicians working with clients who experience negative impacts from **social media use**, this study offers a concrete, evidence-based intervention strategy. Rather than recommending reduced screen time—an approach with limited empirical support—training clients in **wise empathy** (balancing empathic engagement with healthy boundaries) may be more practical and effective. The specific components of wise empathy—**appreciative joy** rather than envy for positive posts, **compassion** rather than personal distress for negative posts, and reduced **upward social comparison**—map onto established therapeutic targets in **CBT** and **compassion-focused therapy**. The EMI design (N = 5,902 observations) provides ecological validity that lab-based studies cannot match. The mediation finding suggests that the mechanism is genuinely about changing how people engage empathically rather than a non-specific intervention effect.

## Methodology Note

This is a quantitative_experimental paper.

__Statistical Power__: N = 260 participants with 5,902 observations provides substantial power for within-person analyses.
The EMI design with multiple observations per person increases effective sample size for detecting within-person effects.
The between-group comparison with N ≈ 130 per condition has adequate power for medium effect sizes.

__Preregistration__: Not mentioned in the available abstract.
The EMI design with experience sampling was pre-specified.

__Effect Sizes__: Specific effect sizes are not available from the abstract/description alone.
The mediation finding (wise empathy mediating the well-being effect) is reported as significant but without specific indirect effect sizes.

__Statistical Rigor__: The **ecological momentary intervention** design with experience sampling is methodologically strong for studying in-situ social media behavior.
The combination of an intervention with naturalistic Instagram browsing provides high ecological validity.
Mediation analysis was used to test the proposed mechanism.
Without the full text, the specific multilevel modeling approach and handling of nested data cannot be fully evaluated.

__Measurement Validity__: Measures included wise empathy components: appreciative joy, positive emotion sharing, compassion, negative emotion sharing, upward social comparisons, and personal distress.
Well-being was measured via experience sampling.
The specific instruments are not named in the abstract.
Experience sampling measures have known advantages (reduced recall bias, ecological validity) but may be affected by reactivity.
